## Background

> Matt Reustle breaks down Cloudflare with Sam Eden of Square Peg's global tech fund. Eden starts with the postal-service analogy: Cloudflare intercepts all inbound web traffic for customers from hobby sites to Shopify merchant storefronts, blocking DDoS attacks, bots, and malicious packets while accelerating delivery through a unified reverse proxy rather than Akamai's fragmented CDN setup.
>
> Founded in 2009 by Matthew Prince, Michelle Zatlin, and Lee Holloway after Project Honeypot built a spammer blocklist, Cloudflare now runs a software-defined network on commodity hardware with peering across 13,000+ networks. Eden walks three acts — application security, zero-trust corporate security, and developer platform including Workers and edge AI inference — plus the reinforcing loop where long-tail traffic lowers bandwidth costs, improves threat data, and pulls in enterprise buyers paying multi-year pool-of-funds contracts.

---

## Key Facts

> **F1** Cloudflare processes over 20% of global web traffic and blocks an average of 2.5 million cyberattacks per second on a single commodity-hardware network peered with 13,000+ networks; annualized revenue exceeds $2 billion with non-GAAP gross margins around 75%–78%, ≈6% of revenue in equipment depreciation compressing reported margins, and capex running 11%–14% of revenue while free-cash-flow margins sit near 10% against 25%+ long-term guidance.

> **F2** Customers above $100,000 ARR are under 1.5% of the base but drive ≈75% of revenue; large-customer revenue growth re-accelerated from ≈30% toward 40% year-over-year after Mark Anderson (ex-Palo Alto Networks president of sales) rebuilt enterprise sales following 2023 rep-productivity struggles, while net retention inflected from 112% to 119% in Q3 2025 aided by pool-of-funds bundling on multi-year contracts.

> **F3** Channel-partner-sourced incremental revenue grew ≈65% year-over-year for two years, rising from ≈20% to over 40% of new sales while total channel revenue is ≈30% versus ≈90% at Zscaler and Netskope; Eden cites a $130 million five-year pool-of-funds deal, RPO growth near 40% through 2025, 55+ revenue products with 10+ product customers the fastest-growing cohort, and 3+ million developers on Act 3 tools including Workers free tiers up to 100,000 daily queries.

## Mental Model · *Single Network Reinforcing Loop*

> **Components**
>
> Intercept all traffic through one reverse proxy — speed, security, CDN, bots — instead of legacy vendors forcing sales engineers to split networks. Free and freemium tiers feed the long tail; aggregated traffic earns ISP peering that cuts bandwidth costs; richer threat signals improve protection and speed; enterprise and pool-of-funds contracts monetize the same hardware across Acts 1–3. Eden stresses all servers run all products, diversifying capex ROI versus building separate networks per product line.
>
> **Application**
>
> Incumbents faced innovator's dilemma: enterprises did not want simple interception, and scaling lookup tables on every customer server was technically brutal. Cloudflare's long-tail PLG created negotiating leverage with ISPs and data advantages competitors cannot replicate quickly. For investors, Eden says valuation requires modeling Act 2 catch-up versus Zscaler and Act 3 AI inference TAM — stock trades near 25x NTM revenue with free-cash-flow margins around 10% today versus 25%+ long-term guidance, so execution must stay flawless.

## Key Insights

> **1.** One proxy beats fragmented CDN-plus-firewall stacks.
>
> **Q** What was Cloudflare's founding breakthrough?
>
> **A** Project Honeypot's bad-actor list could not run on every customer server without slowing the internet. Holloway routed all traffic through Cloudflare first — one mailing address for every packet — so adding CDN or DDoS protection is a toggle, not a new implementation. That unlocked the website long tail Akamai ignored.

> **2.** Act 2 and Act 3 reuse the same metal.
>
> **Q** How did internal security and Workers emerge?
>
> **A** Zero-trust forward proxies inspect outbound employee traffic on existing boxes; developer Workers and storage monetize the same global network with usage pricing and generous free tiers. Eden notes 80% of top AI-native companies reportedly use Cloudflare, and GPUs slot into pre-planned motherboard empty spaces across 330 cities for edge inference.

> **3.** Bundling breaks siloed enterprise buyers.
>
> **Q** What is pool of funds?
>
> **A** Large customers commit multi-year dollars usable across any product — Eden cites a $130 million five-year deal — encouraging experimentation on Workers while mostly funding Act 1. Rolled out in 2024, pool of funds reached low double digits of ACV and helped net retention re-accelerate even though Acts had separate buyers before.

## Chronology

> *Cloudflare*
>
> **Pre-2009** Legacy CDNs like Akamai split networks; enterprises buy separate firewalls and reverse proxies with heavy sales-engineer setup.
>
> **2009** Prince, Zatlin, and Holloway found Cloudflare from Project Honeypot's spammer blocklist and unified traffic interception.
>
> **2010s** Product-led growth serves nonprofits, hackers, and the website long tail on commodity hardware; ISP peering cuts bandwidth costs.
>
> **2020s** Enterprise motion matures; zero-trust Act 2 and developer Workers Act 3 layer onto the same global network.
>
> **2023-2024** Enterprise rep productivity stumbles then recovers under CRO Mark Anderson; pool-of-funds bundling launches in 2024.
