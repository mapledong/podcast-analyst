## Background

> Mitchell Green founded Lead Edge Capital after Bessemer, bringing cold-calling discipline and a practical growth-equity filter to private software. The firm aims for singles and doubles: 2-5x per deal over 3-7 years, 2-2.25x net fund outcomes, and 95% LP gross dollar retention. The episode is packed with operating detail: Lead Edge Eight criteria, 800 mostly executive LPs, 9,000 companies contacted annually, 150-175 diligenced to make 5-7 investments, a disposition committee, and a creative secondary/special-situations engine that now represents around 70% of deployment. Transcript-grounded context: "Ramp saves companies 5%, it's no wonder."

---

## Key Facts

> **F1** Lead Edge talks to about 9,000 companies per year, filters to roughly 900 that meet at least five criteria, diligences 150-175, and does 5-7 deals annually. Its seventh fund was $3.5B. Why have 800 executive LPs instead of 20 big institutions? A former GM CEO can open an automotive software door better than a 22-year-old associate. Because 9,000 companies has to become a manageable strike zone.

> **F2** Deal targets are 2-5x in 3-7 years, around 25% net IRR at the deal level, and 2-2.25x net MOIC with 20% net IRR at the fund level. Funds usually hold about 20 investments, not 100-150. Why have 800 executive LPs instead of 20 big institutions? A former GM CEO can open an automotive software door better than a 22-year-old associate. Because 9,000 companies has to become a manageable strike zone.

> **F3** Lead Edge has about 800 LPs, 95% by number are executives and entrepreneurs. Roughly 70% of recent deployment is creative: special situations, secondary, CVs, derivatives, or public positions. Why have 800 executive LPs instead of 20 big institutions? A former GM CEO can open an automotive software door better than a 22-year-old associate. Because 9,000 companies has to become a manageable strike zone.
