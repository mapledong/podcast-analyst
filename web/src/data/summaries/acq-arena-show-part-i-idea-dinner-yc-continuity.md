## Mental Model · *Inputs Over Outputs at Growth Stage*

> **Components**
>
> Anu Hariharan argues Continuity evaluates founders on operating inputs — three principles consistently applied — rather than trailing revenue or margin snapshots that lag reality. Growth-stage companies with great headline metrics still face existential swings (Airbnb, Tony Xu/Rappi cited). YC's edge is multi-year batch relationships: Continuity sees founders before metrics peak and after near-death. David maps YC to a selective university (hard admission, recurring cohorts) with Continuity as attached growth capital — distinct LP pools per vehicle, not automatic recycling of every exit into one endowment. Idea dinner frames public-market conviction under uncertainty: Ben's Coinbase math ($100 cash at $340) vs FTX derivatives bear case (≈3× volume in derivatives vs spot). Community-dependent businesses (Acquired Slack ≈11,000 members cited elsewhere) carry operating leverage — nurturing community is the core job.
>
> **Application**
>
> Growth investors with accelerator adjacency should weight founder behavior and leading indicators over quarterly dashboards — especially when prior seed relationships exist. For YC alumni, Continuity is slow capital; don't assume automatic follow-on. Public investors can separate narrative ("retail worth zero") from reinvestment math (Amazon capex 3–4× other big tech cited). Live events compound brand but concentrate community risk if mishandled.

## What Makes It Work

> YC's moat is cumulative selection plus network density, not Continuity check size alone. Batch admission remains scarce while output scales — David compares approaching liberal-arts-college graduation scale (1,000–2,000 companies per batch discussed). Alumni market cap (≈$600B PitchBook) and 17-year track record create founder pull competitors cannot replicate quickly. Continuity adds optional growth capital without forcing every company to raise externally — but only ≈2–3 leads per quarter preserves signal quality vs spray-and-pray growth funds. Information advantage: partners know founders from seed through crises — WhatsApp diligence on RevenueCat cited. Post-pandemic, geographic requirement removed (Mountain View no longer mandatory), expanding applicant pool while keeping brand. Weaknesses: Continuity cannot fund every winner; LP structure per fund limits automatic recycling. Public criticism of batch size persists (David notes years of VC complaints). Community-heavy models (YC, Acquired) fear dilution if scale outruns culture. Idea dinner segment is entertainment, not validated alpha — May 2022 picks faced immediate macro headwinds. Versus Tiger/Coatue seed moves or Sequoia permanence reforms, YC pairs earliest access with optional later-stage capital — different from crossover funds analyzed in Altimeter episode.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Snowflake's retention is exceptional; value capture is the debate.
>
> **Q** Why pick Snowflake in a cloud warehouse land grab?
>
> **A** Mario chose Snowflake for sector tailwinds plus what David called record public-company net retention — enterprises moving analytics to cloud warehouses. Ben's pushback: will Snowflake keep capturing value vs AWS Redshift, Databricks, and incumbents? The pick tests whether best-in-class retention survives competitive compression — classic Acquired power vs growth tension.

> **2.** Coinbase looked cheap on cash-flow math in May 2022.
>
> **Q** Why Coinbase over FTX?
>
> **A** Ben framed ≈$340 market cap vs ≈$100 annual cash generation (simplified) plus network effects and brand in crypto trading. FTX bear case: derivatives ≈3× spot volume and FTX better positioned there. Ben liked the pick more in May 2022 than January 2022 after price decline — noting stage investments already made. Exchange economics favor volatility; derivatives share matters for long-term share.

> **3.** Amazon retail losses reflect investment, not zero value.
>
> **Q** Is Amazon retail worthless at a ≈$1.25T cap?
>
> **A** David argued AWS alone (≈$75B+ revenue, high margin) could justify the stock in analyst thought experiments — retail lost ≈$1.5B one quarter because Amazon invests ahead of demand. His spend grew ≈34%/year for five years (230 orders in 12 months). Bezos "what won't change" framing: internet growth → own AWS; e-commerce habit → own retail plus ads, Buy with Prime, cards.

> **4.** Continuity was non-obvious in 2015.
>
> **Q** Why did YC launch a growth fund when accelerators didn't?
>
> **A** Before July 2015, follow-on came from individual angels (Initialize cited) — not YC capital. Fewer than ≈10 investors wrote $100M+ checks; founders needing $100M+ pre-IPO faced supply-demand imbalance. Continuity lets YC stay relevant post-batch without merging admission and growth IC into one committee — partners split roles.

> **5.** Grade growth investing on founder inputs, not snapshots.
>
> **Q** How does Anu pick Continuity winners?
>
> **A** Invest on inputs/leading indicators vs trailing metrics — if founders live three operating principles, high-quality outcomes become more probable even when current dashboards look perfect. David notes growth investors meeting YC companies fresh would underestimate near-death history (Airbnb). Community-dependent organizations must prioritize culture maintenance over scale — David parallels Acquired's Slack community fear.
