## Key Facts

> **F1** Laurene's introduction frames Jobs as seeing what was not there — mind never captive to reality — with 2007 opening quote on free-world entrepreneurs owing deep appreciation through handshake-level love transmission. Archive drafts show stumble, growth, change across NeXT exile, Pixar, and return-era iMac, iPod, and iPhone materials published free in 2023.

> **F2** 1995 Lost Interview: Jobs said observers can tell when work is money-motivated versus care-motivated; 1996 Microsoft praised for aggression but insulted as pedestrian McDonald's-spirit products lacking taste. Land studied from twenties through forties — affusive praise that Polaroid would be immortal until Land died; Jobs kept preaching Land's arts-plus-tech lesson that Senra repeats across episodes 102, 190, and 420.

> **F3** Jobs on rigor: unapologetically extreme work imposed first on himself; teams must be A players with focused no to good ideas. Ive's three-minute clip — most focused person he knew — echoes Land's hour-after-hour concentration releasing unknown capabilities; Senra jokes his alarm clock yells Land like David Goggins yelling to get up.

>
> **Q** How does Polaroid appear?
>
> **A** Jobs studied Land in twenties through forties; built intersection of arts and technology Land pioneered. >
> **Q** Why attack Microsoft?
>
> **A** Jobs called them opportunist third-rate without spirit — McDonald's, not wicked success but pedestrian products. Opposite of making something wonderful: money-first motivation produces visible mediocrity; missionaries build differentiated experiences customers feel. Microsoft 1996 insult: opportunist third-rate pedestrian McDonald's spirit — opposite of making something wonderful.
