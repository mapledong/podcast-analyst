> ### Conclusion
>
> Meituan is China's offline-to-online super-app — food delivery, reviews, hotel/travel, groceries, merchant SaaS — born from the 千团大战 where 5,000+ Groupon clones collapsed to a handful. Wang Xing merged rival Dianping (October 2015), dropped its branding, and built third-largest China tech (≈$270B market cap at recording) on 475M transacting users doing 27 orders/year. Profits scaled from $225M to $1B quarterly operating profit as ads (16% of revenue) and travel offset thin delivery margins. Alibaba's $10B+ Ele.me bet failed (67% vs 30% share by 2019). Tencent's 20% stake and WeChat mini-program traffic are moat and dependency. Lesson: amortize consumer CAC across verticals in dense two-sided markets — but CCP regulatory discretion remains the primary valuation discount.

## Background

> Season 8 Episode 3 (≈143 min) opens with Wang Xing's 1979 birth in Longyan during Deng's reform era, Tsinghua EE degree (2001), and US grad school at University of Delaware. He sold a Facebook clone to Joe Chen for $2M (October 2006) — it became Renren's $740M IPO at ≈$6B market cap (2011). Fanfou, his Twitter clone, hit 2M users before CCP shutdown. March 2010 Meituan launch followed. Parallel track: Tao Jiang founded Dianping in 2003 (predating Yelp 2005) as dish-level restaurant reviews with Google-led Series B. Hosts compare China's mobile-first, BAT-funded proxy wars to fragmented US Yelp/OpenTable/DoorDash stacks. Research credits Tech Buzz China, Evolving for the Next Billion, and Analyse Asia.

## Key Facts

> **F1** China's thousand Groupon war (千团大战) peaked at 5,000 companies with 20–30 new clones launching daily; by end-2011 only Meituan, Dianping, and BAT-backed remnants survived. Wang Xing expanded into tier-2/3 cities while most rivals burned capital in tier-1 coastals — Groupon offered no tech leverage, only subsidized local sales armies signing karaoke bars and massage parlors door-to-door.

> **F2** Meituan raised $12M from Sequoia China at launch (early 2010) and $50M from Alibaba (early 2011) — huge for an era when Series A posts were ≈$6M. Dianping received Sequoia China's $1.5M in 2006 — a cash-flow-positive review business versus capital-hungry foot soldiers. Meituan's January 2015 round added $700M; Ele.me raised $630M in August 2015 just two months before the October 8, 2015 merger announcement.

> **F3** Meituan-Dianping merger ended a Tencent-Alibaba proxy war. Alibaba sold its combined stake for $900M, then invested $1.25B into Ele.me for 25% and bought the rest for $9.5B (April 2018) — over $10B total Alibaba capital in the category. Food delivery couriers literally fought in streets; China's delivery market is ≈4× North America's size growing ≈30% annually.

> **F4** September 2018 Hong Kong IPO raised ≈$3B at a $50B market cap (up from ≈$30B prior round). 2019: $15B revenue, $1B operating cash flow, net income positive. Meituan reached 67% food delivery share by 2019 (Ele.me 30%); 600M active users and 10M transactions/day by March 2017. Travel launched ≈5 years before episode and hit 46% China share versus Ctrip's 20%.

> **F5** At recording (~March 2021): ≈$270B market cap — third-largest China tech behind Tencent (≈$850B) and Alibaba (≈$675B), having run from $65B in May 2020 to $300B by February 2021. 475M transacting users averaged 27 transactions/year across 6.5M merchants; quarterly operating profit jumped from $225M to $1B. Merchant ads are 16% of revenue. Tencent owns 20%; ≈50% of customers use the WeChat mini program.

## Mental Model · *Density Before Diversity*

> **Components**
>
> Win local commerce by proving city-level density — riders, merchants, users — before national breadth. Wang Xing survived 千团大战 by targeting tier-2/3 mobile-first cities while rivals subsidized tier-1 coastals. Groupon clones added $100M revenue tranches without margin improvement; Tao Jiang publicly predicted the category would die. The October 2015 Dianping merger ended dual subsidy war under Tao's rule: two players means nobody profits. Meituan then amortized acquisition cost across verticals — food delivery as consumer wedge, then hotel/travel (46% share within ≈5 years), local services, groceries, ads (16% of revenue), and merchant SaaS/lending. Operating profit scaled from $225M/quarter to $1B/quarter once the cross-sell flywheel engaged. Real-world services penetration was only ≈5% in 2017 versus ≈20% e-commerce saturation — headroom for O2O winners.
>
> **Application**
>
> Multi-city marketplace founders should prove unit economics in one dense market, not Groupon-style land grabs where every city requires a new sales army. Super-app economics require shared logistics, ads, and payment rails — not app-icon bundling. US DoorDash did ≈$2.9B 2020 net revenue versus Meituan's ≈$15B in 2019, yet both trade near 14–16× revenue; Meituan's 18-year Dianping review database and 27 transactions/user/year density explain the gap Western siloed apps cannot close quickly.

## What Makes It Work

> Meituan stacks Hamilton Helmer scale economies atop an 18-year Dianping review database — dish-level ratings, photos, reservations, and average check prices Western Yelp never integrated with delivery. 475M transacting users let fixed costs of new verticals amortize Netflix-style: community group buying, grocery hubs, travel. Delivery network density hit 67% share by 2019; merchant ads contribute 16% of revenue at near-100% gross margin once traffic exists on-property. B2B SaaS and merchant lending (Square Capital analog) add high-margin layers. Wang Xing's serial-clone pattern recognition and militaristic courier ops culture — investor-relations video shows lined-up riders with commanders — outlasted 5,000 Groupon burners. Tencent owns 20% and routes ≈50% of customers through WeChat mini programs; traffic is moat and dependency. Weaknesses: Alibaba pumped $10B+ into Ele.me yet held only 30% share. Food delivery margins stay thin; Q1 2020 revenue fell 12% when travel — the highest-margin segment — collapsed. Community group buying pits Meituan against Pinduoduo on the next frontier. CCP regulatory discretion is real — Fanfou shutdown and Jack Ma precedent — and US analogues lack super-app payment rails.

## Key Insights

> **1.** 千团大战 was venture bonfire.
>
> **Q** Why 5,000 Groupon clones?
>
> **A** Late 2009–2011 China VC frenzy copied US models faster than unit economics could prove. 5,000 companies launched with 20–30 new clones daily; Groupon itself entered via Tencent JV (Gao Peng). The business needed local sales armies per city — no tech leverage, terrible merchant churn. Wang Xing survived by expanding tier-2/3 cities while rivals fought tier-1 coastals. By end-2011 only Meituan, Dianping, and BAT remnants remained. Groupon's IPO became the high-water mark — market cap down 90% within 8–10 months.

> **2.** Merge with rival beats price war.
>
> **Q** Why Dianping merger?
>
> **A** Tao Jiang told GGV: with two or three players nobody makes money; merge unless you can kill the rival. Meituan and Dianping talked for ≈2 years before October 8, 2015 announcement — Ele.me had just raised $630M and lost its Dianping product integration overnight. Alibaba sold its stake for $900M and backed Ele.me with $1.25B; Tencent and Sequoia won as largest shareholders of the combined entity. Review touchpoints gave Dianping cheaper consumer acquisition than subsidized Groupon armies.

> **3.** Food is consumer land-and-expand wedge.
>
> **Q** How make money on thin take rates?
>
> **A** Hosts frame food as go-to-market for all consumer services — enterprise-style land-and-expand in consumer. Delivery acquires users; hotel/travel (46% China share in ≈5 years), local services, groceries, and 16% ad revenue monetize at higher margin. 475M users × 27 transactions/year across 6.5M merchants amortize CAC. Operating profit jumped from $225M to $1B per quarter once cross-sell clicked. Meituan at ≈$15B 2019 revenue is 5–7× DoorDash's ≈$2.9B 2020 net revenue.

> **4.** Wang Xing is China's serial pattern matcher.
>
> **Q** Founder advantage?
>
> **A** Facebook clone sold for $2M became Renren ($740M IPO, ≈$6B cap). Fanfou hit 2M Twitter users before CCP shutdown taught regulatory limits. Meituan was clone #3 — March 2010 with $12M Sequoia and $50M Alibaba. He copied US models faster but adapted to mobile leapfrog and super-app reality. BBS-era peers included Pony Ma, Jack Ma, Colin Huang. Tier-2/3 expansion insight and ruthless capital efficiency separated survivors from 5,000 Groupon burners.

> **5.** Tencent traffic is moat and dependency.
>
> **Q** Investment risk?
>
> **A** Tencent owns 20%; ≈50% of customers use WeChat mini programs — traffic Tencent controls. Fractured relationship would hurt acquisition but 18-year review scale is hard to replicate. Bigger discount: CCP regulatory discretion (antitrust, gig rules, common-prosperity). Episode recorded at ≈$270B cap before post-2021 crackdowns. Business economics proved durable — profits, 67% delivery share — but policy remains primary valuation variable versus US antitrust framing.
