## Key Facts

> **F1** Michael Mauboussin leads Consilient Research at Counterpoint Global (Morgan Stanley Investment Management); mid-2021 Counterpoint Global managed approximately $180 billion AUM after joining from Credit Suisse/Legg Mason arc. Hosts cite his Google 2012 'Untangling Skill and Luck' talk as gateway to his research franchise and Capital Camp intro via Patrick O'Shaughnessy and Brent Beshore.

> **F2** Paradox of skill (Stephen Jay Gould via Ted Williams): as absolute skill rises, relative gaps shrink — Ted Williams hit .406 in 1941 (≈3 standard deviations) while modern .390 tops batting titles; beer-league hockey looks random because players are equally skilled, not unskilled. Bill Miller beat the S&P 500 for 15 years — Mauboussin argues that streak may never repeat as alpha distributions compress.

> **F3** Russell 3000 intangible investments equaled capex (≈$630–640 billion each) in 2001; Mauboussin projects 2021 intangibles at ≈$2 trillion versus ≈$1 trillion capex — accounting understates software-era investment. Stripe CEO John Carlson podcast noted expensing customer acquisition as SG&A while building durable value; Walmart's first 15 public years showed negative free cash flow each year while profitable on the income statement.

> **F4** Expectations Investing (Mauboussin + Al Rappaport, revised 2021) reverse-engineers implied growth from price — first edition published September 10, 2001 into dot-com bust. Three steps: ask what you must believe, compare to strategic analysis probabilistically, act only on differential view. John Burr Williams' 1938 DCF text already advocated reverse-engineering price for skeptics.

> **F5** October 2021 ≈1.3–1.4% 10-year Treasury inflates embedded expectations; first $1T market cap took all human history, then ≈18 months produced multiple $2T companies. Top-10 snapshot: only Microsoft in both 2001 and 2021 lists — other nine 2001 leaders down ≈$460 billion combined; Apple rose from <$10B to ≈$2.4T, Amazon from ≈$7B to ≈$1.6T; 3 of 2021 top 10 were not public in 2001, 2 not yet founded; GE was 2001 #1.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Paradox of skill hides narrowing edges.
>
> **Q** Why do outcomes feel randomer now?
>
> **A** Absolute skill rises — tools, training, global talent pools — but relative gaps shrink. Ted Williams .406 (1941) was ≈3 sigma; .390 wins today. Active manager alpha bell curves skinnier — markets efficient because skillful. Buffett's streak needed skill plus luck; replication harder at scale.

> **2.** Price embeds expectations not value.
>
> **Q** How think about 2021 multiples?
>
> **A** Reverse-engineer price first — Burr Williams, 1938. Rappaport: executives must exceed expectations embedded in stock. ≈1.3% 10-year Treasury plus growth drove $1T-to-$2T sprint in 18 months. Pass when no differential view; intangibles ($2T projected) make earnings less informative than cash.

> **3.** Moats are measurable not mystical.
>
> **Q** What does Measuring the Moat add?
>
> **A** Quantifies ROIC spread persistence and industry structure versus qualitative 7 Powers. Walmart first 15 public years: negative free cash flow while income-statement profitable — investing in intangibles accounting misses. Unit economics beat book value when 3 of 2021 top 10 were not public in 2001.

> **4.** VC top firms may feed themselves.
>
> **Q** Is persistence skill or access?
>
> **A** Preferential attachment: winning deals improves flow and returns — hard to separate from picking skill. Star partner leaving top firm tests hypothesis: Mauboussin suspects returns do not port cleanly. Public equities show limited persistence; venture top 10–20% still persistent.

> **5.** Weak players should increase variance.
>
> **Q** Success Equation strategy?
>
> **A** Strong player simplifies to overwhelm (chess vs Magnus). Weak player runs trick plays — startups vs incumbents. Luck-skill continuum sets regression speed: lotteries fully regress, chess none. Poker table selection: find neglected non-index names or frontier markets where you are smartest player.
