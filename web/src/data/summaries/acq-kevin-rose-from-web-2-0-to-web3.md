## Key Insights

> **1.** Digg founding lore was wrong in Wikipedia.
>
> **Q** Was Kevin inspired by lunch with Woz?
>
> **A** Business Week cover story claimed Woz lunch triggered Digg; Kevin says CmdrTaco (Slashdot) on Screen Savers and Jay Adelson collaboration were real triggers — guest-on-show → co-founder path, not a single meal epiphany. Episode ties to broader Acquired themes: concentrated ownership, DPI over IRR marketing, and founder quality sensed before metrics confirm. The episode ties numbers to primary sources (Chernow, Russo, Chang interviews, company filings where cited) rather than secondary hype.

> **2.** Web 2.0 naming lagged the movement.
>
> **Q** What did Web 2.0 mean to Kevin?
>
> **A** Ajax, dynamic user-submitted content, social discovery — practitioners lived it years before the label. Same as NFT artists making art before 'NFT' category existed; deepest participants rarely coin the buzzword.

> **3.** Mobile/social were extensions, not new paradigms.
>
> **Q** Why is Web3 different from ML/mobile waves?
>
> **A** Instagram=Flickr, WhatsApp=AIM — 1:1 analogues. Web3 builders create because they're in community ('thing needs to exist'), not slide-deck seams. Kevin calls this first true new wave since Digg era.

> **4.** Paradigm shifts require leap-of-faith adoption.
>
> **Q** Why do early believers win?
>
> **A** Parents warned 'don't put personal info on Facebook' — same energy as NFT skeptics. First movers take reputation risk; Kevin's June 2011 Bitcoin tests and Beeple episodes preceded mainstream NFT clicks for Ben.

> **5.** Content-commerce intertwining is under-copied.
>
> **Q** What model does Hodinkee prove?
>
> **A** Hodinkee enriches purchasable goods (watches) with content — half media, half commerce. Kevin aims Proof/MoFi at NFT-native version: on-chain media, not just MP3 uploads. Watches preview scarce digital goods economics.
