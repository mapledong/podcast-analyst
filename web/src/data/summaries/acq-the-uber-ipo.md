## Key Facts

> **F1** Uber IPO May 10, 2019: priced $45/share; opened ≈$42, down ≈7% from open on first day per Ben and David recording live — longest Acquired history tour preceding IPO-day analysis.

> **F2** Uber raised over $20 billion cumulatively pre-IPO; IPO added approximately $8 billion primary — Ben compares to roughly half of Lyft's entire ≈$15 billion market cap at its IPO.

> **F3** 2017 SoftBank tender offered ≈$48 billion valuation versus prior $68 billion post-DiDi round — cleaned cap table, dropped Benchmark lawsuit, Travis reduced role; SoftBank bought up to ≈15% of company.

> **F4** Uber S-1 equity stakes cited: ≈15% DiDi (China), ≈38% Yandex Taxi (Russia), ≈23% Grab (Southeast Asia) — ≈$18 billion aggregate 'holding company' value per Ben, roughly 20% of Uber's value.

> **F5** Ride-hailing revenue growth decelerated: ≈100% (2016–2017) → ≈42% (2017–2018) → roughly flat recent quarters pre-IPO per bear narrative; Garrett Camp's 2007 StumbleUpon sale to eBay was $75 million.

## Mental Model · *Global Ambition, Local Network Effects*

> **Components**
>
> Uber assumed winner-take-all global ride-sharing; reality is nationally fragmented markets without cross-border network effects — Ben calls it 'a series of markets.' Capital as weapon (SoftBank, Saudis, Fidelity June 2014 crossover rounds) delayed IPO but didn't settle US/LATAM subsidy wars versus Lyft. Marketplace playbook (supply acquisition, surge, city launch playbooks) works locally; international 'merge not compete' (DiDi swap) admits limits. Travis's Scour trauma — MPAA $250 billion suit scare, Michael Ovitz — shaped aggression that arrived as 2017 culture cost.
>
> **Application**
>
> When evaluating super-app or marketplace TAM, test whether network effects cross geographies or require re-fighting supply/demand per city. Governance and culture debt compound — Susan Fowler blog, Holder Report, Benchmark suing Travis show operational excellence can't outrun HR/legal blowback. Dara Khosrowshahi's peacemaker role is part of bull case; $9B IPO raise may be last capital injection.

---

## What Makes It Work

> Uber's strengths: first-mover brand, dense driver/rider liquidity in top cities, multi-product leverage (Rides + Eats shares driver infrastructure per bull case), and strategic stakes in consolidated foreign markets. Travis-era city launch playbooks (Brian Tolkin LP guest) and Benchmark/Gurley board capital access created template startups copied worldwide. Weaknesses: subsidies required vs Lyft; AV timeline uncertain; Otto/Waymo legal hangover; driver classification regulation; $20B+ capital raised sets high exit hurdle; ride-sharing not globally unified network — DiDi/Grab ownership is admission of defeat in all-out conquest.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Uber opened the stay-private floodgates.
>
> **Q** Why IPO in 2019 after $20B raised?
>
> **A** Uber pioneered Fidelity/Tiger/SoftBank private rounds — Fidelity's June 2014 crossover changed startup financing. Eventually employees and LPs need liquidity; Uber still needed ≈$8B primary while Slack/Zoom DPOs needed none. Era of A+ IPOs clustered as late-stage funds exhausted patience after $20B+ cumulative raises. Ben: 'This whole era of new capital sources was opened up really by Uber.'

> **2.** Travis's Scour trauma shaped Uber aggression.
>
> **Q** Why so much Uber backstory?
>
> **A** Michael Ovitz lawsuit, MPAA $250 billion suit scare against Scour, Red Swoosh sale to Akamai — Travis learned hardball early. Garrett's James Bond/black-car insight (Casino Royale phone-summoned car) met operator willing to fight regulators and Lyft simultaneously. Raised $260M at $3.7B post after 10x from $350M round — multifront war capital. Culture cost arrived 2017 with Susan Fowler and Holder Report.

> **3.** #DeleteUber misfired but fuel was real.
>
> **Q** JFK surge controversy — what happened?
>
> **A** Correction from prior episode: Uber turned surge OFF during JFK taxi strike protest, not on — but years of driver and consumer ill will made any spark combustible. Susan Fowler blog weeks later was separate catalyst for Holder Report. #DeleteUber misfired as narrative but reflected real fuel from Travis-era decisions.

> **4.** 20% of Uber is a fund of taxis.
>
> **Q** Bull case on international?
>
> **A** Instead of winning every market, Uber holds ≈$18B in Didi/Grab/Yandex stakes — roughly 20% of company value per Ben. Call option on consolidated foreign ride-share plus US core. Bear: stakes illiquid; US still subsidized versus Lyft. Otto acquisition cost ≈$650M and looked like buying Google trade secrets — Waymo scandal compounded governance overhang.

> **5.** Dara's peace is the bull case hinge.
>
> **Q** Five-year A+ scenario?
>
> **A** Contribution-margin-positive rides plus Eats sharing supply-side spend — knife fight ends when subsidies stop under Lyft public pressure. Bull structural advantage: keep driver busy 100% between Eats and rides, reducing multihoming incentive on Lyft. F scenario: capital markets close before unit economics work (Dara's ≈$9B IPO raise may be last injection); AV never arrives to justify $20B+ raised over 10 years.
