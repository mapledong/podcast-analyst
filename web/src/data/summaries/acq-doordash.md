## Background

> Tony Xu, Stanley Tang, Andy Fang, and Evan Moore start Palo Alto Delivery in Stanford's Design Garage class (2012–13), inspired by Square's smartphone-enabled merchants and absent suburban delivery outside pizza chains. Grubhub/Seamless owned NYC with restaurant-employed couriers — capital-light but geographically limited. DoorDash owned fulfillment with 1099 drivers and $6 flat fees. YC summer 2013 → $2.4M seed (Keith Rabois/Khosla). Series B: John Doerr, $40M at $600M (2015). SoftBank $535M at $1.4B (38% dilution), 2017 $60M Sequoia bridge, recovery to $600M at $12.6B (2019). Caviar acquired from Square. S-1 reveals brutal dilution while share rose 17%→50% 2018–2020. Recorded alongside Airbnb IPO week — Alfred Lin's second Sequoia portfolio IPO in two days. Grubhub passed orders to restaurant couriers; DoorDash owned suburban 1099 fulfillment — category insight that unlocked national Yum! chain partnerships after Peninsula land-grab success.

## Key Facts

> **F1** DoorDash US market share grew from 17% (January 2018) to 50% (October 2020) — surpassing Uber Eats, Grubhub, and Postmates in roughly 2¾ years per S-1 analysis cited in episode.

> **F2** 2019 metrics: $885 million net revenue (more than 3X year-on-year), $8 billion gross order value, 263 million orders, and 60% same-store sales growth excluding new markets and restaurant additions.

> **F3** SoftBank invested $535 million in 2018 at $1.4 billion post-money valuation — 38% dilution with per-share price still below John Doerr's 2015 Series B at $600 million post despite higher headline valuation.

> **F4** By early 2014 one in six San Francisco Bay Peninsula residents had used DoorDash — roughly one year after first paloaltodelivery.com delivery; Tony Xu, Stanley Tang, Andy Fang started in Stanford Design Garage class (2012–13) with $6 flat delivery fees.

> **F5** DashPass membership reached 5 million subscribers at $10 per month — ≈$600 million annual run-rate if fully paid. Caviar acquired from Square for ≈$410M expanded premium segment. Sequoia provided $60M insider bridge end-2017 when cash exhausted; Alfred Lin committed up to $40M pro-rata at $1B cap before SoftBank round.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Suburbs beat cities for delivery ops.
>
> **Q** Why launch San Jose not SF?
>
> **A** Dense cities mean parking tickets, restaurant queues, high-rise drops — killing throughput versus San Jose suburban routes. Tony: consumers remember 53-minute delivery tails not 35-minute means. Peninsula land-grab hit one-in-six residents by early 2014 within ~one year of first paloaltodelivery.com delivery. Early Find My Friends iPhone tracking was hacky real-time ops before dedicated logistics stack — Square internship DNA informed merchant onboarding.

> **2.** Fulfillment ownership was the category insight.
>
> **Q** How was Grubhub different?
>
> **A** Grubhub/Seamless (profitable, public since early 2000s) passed orders to restaurant-employed couriers — worked in NYC bike density, not Palo Alto sprawl. DoorDash 1099 drivers unlocked suburbs and national chains like Yum! (Taco Bell, KFC). Started with $6 flat delivery fee in Stanford Design Garage class (2012–13); DashPass $10/month now reduces checkout friction for 5M members (≈$600M run-rate if fully paid).

> **3.** SoftBank round was dilution shock then catalyst.
>
> **Q** What happened in 2018?
>
> **A** $535M at $1.4B post sold 38% — per-share price below 2015 Kleiner Series B at $600M post despite higher headline valuation. Funded share war vs Uber Eats and Postmates; Meituan China proof gave SoftBank conviction after US food-delivery skepticism. March 2019 raised $250M at less than 5% dilution — leverage returned after competitors stumbled. Caviar $410M from Square expanded premium segment alongside core suburban routes.

> **4.** Sequoia conviction through bridges.
>
> **Q** Who saved the company in 2017?
>
> **A** S-1 revealed $60M insider bridge when cash exhausted end of 2017 — Sequoia kept DoorDash alive pre-SoftBank round. Alfred Lin committed up to $40M pro-rata at $1B cap before external pricer. Built ≈18% IPO stake through bridges when Sand Hill skeptics passed on food delivery — Alfred Lin's second Sequoia portfolio IPO in two days alongside Airbnb that same December 2020 week.

> **5.** Pandemic was hyper-growth on huge base.
>
> **Q** How big was COVID bump?
>
> **A** March 2020 delivered 20%+ GBV growth in one month off $8B+ prior-year GOV — seed-stage velocity at scale rarely seen. Share rose 17%→50% by October 2020 while Uber Eats grew slower. 2019 baseline: $885M net revenue (3X YoY), 263M orders, 60% same-store sales growth. DashPass 5M members at $10/month; Prop 22 preserved California 1099 model underpinning variable labor economics.
