## Key Insights

> **1.** Coding is AGI Act II, not a vertical feature.
>
> **Q** Why does Guangmi elevate coding above other AI use cases?
>
> **A** At difficulty 8–9/10, coding is the hardest mainstream knowledge task — solving it implies cascading automation across ≈90% of desk work. Sonnet 4.5–4.6 delivered more Q1 progress than all of 2025; 70–80% AI-written code at frontier labs is a phase change, not incremental copilot. Models-as-OS means the winner sets APIs, harness defaults, and training data loops for everything downstream.

> **2.** Anthropic may win the ARR race among power users.
>
> **Q** How should investors read OpenAI versus Anthropic revenue?
>
> **A** Top one to two million users drive disproportionate token spend — hundreds of dollars daily for coding power users. Guangmi sees Anthropic ARR potentially exceeding OpenAI in that cohort, with combined frontier ARR at $80–100B year-end and $200B+ next year. Bet on whoever compounds coding feedback data, not headline user counts.

> **3.** Leadership rotates every ≈100 days — moats are flywheels.
>
> **Q** Does the big-three rotation kill durable moats?
>
> **A** Benchmark leadership swaps roughly quarterly, but coding agents create self-improving loops — real fix signals retrain models. Once a lab captures engineer workflow data at scale, catch-up resembles carriage-versus-car dynamics. Harness layers outside raw models (sandboxes, checkpoints) may capture value even as APIs commoditize.
