## Key Facts

> **F1** JC Penney employed 1,600 stores generating $300 million revenue when Walton joined in 1940 at $85 a month. Penney wrapped a customer's package and showed managers a dime of string ate profit — Walton watched costs 24/7. At 27 he raised $25,000 from banker father-in-law L.S. Robson for a Newport Ben Franklin in a town of 4,000; popcorn and ice cream machines packed weekends until landlord Benjamin Harrison refused renewal — attorney confirmed no five-year option — wiping out everything at 32.

> **F2** Helen refused pity: Bentonville restart with 99-year leases, knocking walls for barber traffic. Ozark commutes sparked charter flights — 90 minutes versus eight-hour mountain drives — letting Walton surprise stores competitors could not reach. Rogers 16,000 sq ft discount store grossed $700,000 with donkey rides and 115-degree watermelon promotions David Glass first called foolishness then joined; 13,000 sq ft variety experiments and hula-hoop loads via 12-foot johnboat showed high-agency merchandising.

> **F3** Kmart's Harry Cunningham dominated until Walton flew to Sol Price January 1983, saw 10% membership model, opened Sam's Club by April — eight months to first calendar opening. IBM mainframe for $500 linked 36 departments daily; Walton climbed 18-wheeler cabs at 2:30 a.m. with donuts on loading docks. Bezos handed employees Walton's autobiography; family wealth later approached a quarter-trillion dollars across heirs who avoided massive gift taxes via staged ranch equity games.

## Mental Model · *Buy Cheap, Sell Low, Never Stop Visiting*

> **Components**
>
> Walton maximized price advantage and miles traveled while minimizing packaging string, lease ambiguity, and headquarters comfort. Paul Graham's bitterness-versus-determination frame fits: depression divorce and father's struggles did not stop an Eagle Scout quarterback from grinding. Copy Kmart, Price, and Febles chocolate store walks shamelessly — Bezos flying 17 hours to visit retailers — while out-executing with MBWA, profit-sharing bonuses up to 25%, and Patton-like promotion stunts competitors dismissed until volume proved them.
>
> **Application**
>
> Leaders belong in stores or trucks, not conference rooms — charter planes if geography blocks you. Read lease renewal clauses twice; Newport loss at 32 was recoverable only because Helen insisted on rebuilding. Cut blackjack side bets: Walton lost two years diversifying into shopping centers. Wire warehouses early — $500 IBM bet beat peers who waited — and treat watermelon stains as marketing tuition, not embarrassment.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Simplicity hides ferocious discipline.
>
> **Q** What was Walton's core idea?
>
> **A** Buy cheap, sell low, do it every day with a smile — Charlie Munger's winning systems go ridiculously far maximizing or minimizing one variable. Underneath: undefeated high school quarterback, Eagle Scout drive, depression-era refusal to waste a minute. Munger on winning systems going ridiculously far on one variable maps to buy cheap sell low. Paul Graham bitterness-versus-determination: Walton rose above depression-era divorce through work not IQ alone.

> **2.** Failure accelerates if you keep moving.
>
> **Q** What happened at Newport?
>
> **A** Missing a five-year renewal option on a handshake deal cost everything at 32 — Helen refused pity; they restarted in Bentonville. Paul Graham's bitterness-versus-determination frame: Walton rose above depression divorce and father's struggles through work ethic, not IQ alone. Blackjack shopping-center detour lost two years — split focus lesson. Hula hoops financed via johnboat and 12-foot loads showed high-agency merchandising competitors mocked until volume proved them.

> **3.** Technology and frugality compound.
>
> **Q** How did Walmart scale logistics?
>
> **A** Distribution centers looked backward until drivers proved the model; $500 IBM bet connected warehouses daily. Walton flew recklessly, visited competitors including chocolate companies 500 miles off route, and treated frontline workers like Bezos later would — nickname slave driver, affable boy underneath. Ron Mayer succession drama: Walton retired then couldn't stay away — Old Habits scornfully returned. Rob Walton $5,000 ranch game taught heirs equity without gift-tax hits — family wealth later near quarter-trillion.
