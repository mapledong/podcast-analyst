## Background

> Part I (2018) covered Sequoia seed and internal Series A when Rec Room had hundreds of thousands of users and zero revenue. Origin story: 30+ Microsoft HoloLens team members left as Against Gravity with no plan; Rec Room emerged from social VR experiments. ## Key Facts

> **F1** Rec Room reported ≈660% growth in 2020 during COVID — Nick attributes mix to cross-platform accessibility, creator-led discovery, and pandemic congregation needs, not VR headset sales alone (Quest 2 launched late 2020).

> **F2** Roblox traded near ≈$40 billion market cap at March 2021 direct listing versus ≈$4 billion a year prior; Fortnite/Epic rumored raising at ≈$28 billion — comp set for Rec Room's social-UGC economics.

> **F3** Rec Token economy: users buy tokens upfront; creators earn via selling experiences/items and can cash out — generating float similar to Roblox while ≈40% of revenue shares to creators per Nick's discussion.

> **F4** Launching on Xbox in 2019 doubled Rec Room's user base in one week — illustrating platform expansion impact when core product is free and cross-platform.

> **F5** Company scaled to roughly 60–70 employees at Series C after ≈$15 million from Sequoia (seed plus unannounced internal A) — capital-efficient versus Roblox headcount at comparable hype phase.

## Mental Model · *One World, Many Screens*

> **Components**
>
> Rec Room rejects forked VR/flat clients. VR-first input (physical punch vs button) preserved on mobile via hard translation — harder engineering, stronger moat. Growth decoupled from Oculus install base by shipping iOS, Xbox, and PSVR. UGC flywheel: high creator conversion versus classic 1% rule — creators build haunted houses, sell flashlights, earn Rec Tokens, recruit players (Convoy marketplace liquidity analogy). Token inflation managed like central bank: soft → hard currency transition with economist-minded PM oversight. Platform risk lesson: venture-funded companies cannot bet entirely on another platform's adoption curve.
>
> **Application**
>
> Consumer social products should default to single persistent world if cross-platform social density is the asset — splitting shards kills liquidity. When 90% of value is creator-led, subsidize creator behaviors before paid UA. Evaluate metaverse bets on cross-platform DAU and monetization depth, not headset CAGR alone.

## What Makes It Work

> Rec Room stacks UGC social powers distinct from Roblox and Fortnite. Network economies with high coefficient: David argues creator-user loop multiplier exceeds typical social nets because Rec Room converts users to creators at unusually high rates — each creator adds experiences attracting more participants (7 Powers live with CEO). Switching costs for creators: sunk build effort in one world — like podcast audience migration cost Ben/David cite — deters platform hops. Counter-positioning vs Roblox: integrated creation without separate studio app; targets older demographic Roblox cannot age into without alienating core kids — Fortnite uses buttons not VR physics. Process/UX power: VR-native interactions on flat screens differentiate from ported games; third-person streaming camera for Twitch built early. Weaknesses: Platform store dependence; token economy gaming risk; metaverse hype cyclicality; Roblox scale advantage; Nick acknowledges stagnation if creator flywheel stalls (B- scenario).

## Key Insights

> **1.** Door number three saved Rec Room — VR-only or flat fork would have failed.
>
> **Q** What was the Kobayashi Maru choice?
>
> **A** Pure VR meant zero-percent growth capped by headset installs. Flat fork with traditional mobile controls would forfeit VR-native social physics and one-world liquidity. Nick chose unified world with hard engineering to map VR interactions to touch — enabling Xbox launch that doubled users in one week while preserving metaverse density.

> **2.** Creator-led growth replaces performance marketing.
>
> **Q** Why skip heavy user acquisition spend?
>
> **A** Successful creators market experiences — pinball rooms, haunted houses — recruiting friends. David compares to Convoy's marketplace liquidity: more participants improve match quality. Rec Room captures ≈60% of token economy (40% to creators) while creators absorb marketing labor — capital-efficient at ≈60–70 headcount.

> **3.** Rec Tokens add economic layer to network effects.
>
> **Q** How do tokens change the flywheel?
>
> **A** Evolution: items only → soft currency for behaviors → hard currency users buy and creators cash out. Float from prepaid tokens aids cash flow (Roblox underappreciated angle). Creators decide reinvest versus withdraw — GameStop arbitrage jokes aside, real labor markets emerge inside the world.

> **4.** Counter-positioning is demographic and product architecture.
>
> **Q** How is Rec Room different from Roblox for 7 Powers?
>
> **A** Roblox separate creator app and younger skew — Rec Room integrates tools in-world and serves older users Roblox cannot chase without destroying kid-safe brand. Counter-positioning: Roblox copying integrated VR-native physics would be value-destructive to their mobile kid UX.

> **5.** Fundraising inverts when product works — plan optional.
>
> **Q** How did Series C differ from seed?
>
> **A** Seed was story on speculative VR; Series C arrived eight months into pandemic with investors inbound, high margins after monetization, and optional use-of-proceeds — Nick notes may go public with cash still on balance sheet (Zoom analogy). IRR outperforms despite longer journey because outcome quality rose.
