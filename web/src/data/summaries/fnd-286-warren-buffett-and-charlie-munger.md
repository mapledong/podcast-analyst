## Background

> Senra reads Peter Bevelin's All I Want to Know Is Where I'm Going to Die, So I'll Never Go There — a conversational compilation of Buffett and Munger quotes from Berkshire meetings and biographies. Recommended by Eric Jorgenson, it presents four characters in a library of wisdom. Themes repeat: mistakes are normal, prevention beats cure, focus beats multitasking, inversion finds what to avoid, and compound interest needs time and a good wave.

## Key Facts

> **F1** Munger: wisdom is prevention; Buffett: it wasn't raining when Noah built the ark. Berkshire has no meetings or committees; Buffett reads constantly and targets three good decisions a year while Bezos targets three good decisions a day after eight hours sleep.

> **F2** Inversion runs the book: study business death, collect bad judgment instances, engineer failure out. Compound interest snowball — Berkshire was small once; surf Microsoft or Intel waves. Singleton got facts then thought in his Apple II office; Buffett refuses others' opinions.

> **F3** Debt kills via leverage zeros; cash is oxygen — roughly $100 billion earning 4% risk-free cited. Envy not greed drives misery — someone always richer faster. Sol Price membership fees excluded bad-check customers; passion not money is first hiring filter Buffett shares with Bezos missionaries line.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Multitasking taxes cognition.
>
> **Q** Why do Buffett and Munger reject busy schedules?
>
> **A** Munger succeeded because of long attention span, not IQ edge. Multitasking crowds out deep thought — Edwin Land and Jobs agreed intense concentration releases hidden ability. Bezos needs eight hours sleep for three high-quality decisions daily; Buffett targets three per year. Bezos picked up schedule-time-to-think from this ethos — eight hours sleep, three high-quality decisions daily versus Buffett's three per year. Multitasking generations will underperform solitary readers Munger predicts.

> **2.** Most disasters repeat because people ignore history.
>
> **Q** How should founders use biographies?
>
> **A** Buffett: learn most lessons from others' mistakes. Munger notes little originality in common disasters — drunk driving, cults, repeated business failures. Senra hears direct endorsement of studying effective individuals and financial history, the Founders podcast thesis. Inversion examples repeat: study business decline before success; turn everything into reverse to find what you like by eliminating what you don't. Buffett on Singleton: crime that business schools ignore Teledyne.

> **3.** Cash and frugality enable survival.
>
> **Q** What kills rich people?
>
> **A** Munger: liquor, ladies, leverage. Buffett: bright people go broke on borrowed money; life snaps at the weakest link. Berkshire hoards Treasury bills — more money lost reaching for yield than at gunpoint — to stay in the game when others get foolish. Three I's — innovators, imitators, idiots — explain finance bubbles Munger satirized with sheep joke. Brand is a promise; reputation over time cannot be faked — Gianni Agnelli line Buffett repeats.
