## Background

> Episode 16 on Scripps/Midroll/Stitcher trifecta — old broadcast media buying podcast stack. EW Scripps operates cable networks and local TV/radio stations, historically newspapers. Midroll ad network (≈30% take; handful of shows >$1M gross). Stitcher client with ≈8M registered users (≈$0.56/user acquisition cost). Industry fragmented: Squarespace RSS, Libsyn hosting, manual Midroll sales minimums. Total U.S. podcast ad spend ≈$35M/year (≈2% YoY) — entire TAM smaller than Midroll price. Ben's PSL podcast ad dynamic insertion research; Apple owns directory but ignores monetization. John Gruber and Ben Thompson warn of Facebook-ization. Midroll launches Howl premium originals tier post-Stitcher deal.

## Key Facts

> **F1** Total U.S. podcast advertising spend was roughly $35 million per year at episode recording according to a Wall Street Journal citation — only roughly 2% year-over-year growth — meaning the entire industry TAM was smaller than Scripps's roughly $50 million Midroll purchase price alone. Ben explored a PSL dynamic ad insertion startup but passed for MVP heaviness versus Instagram.

> **F2** Scripps paid roughly $50 million for Midroll Media on roughly $1.5–2 million in annual revenue (roughly 30% take rate; a handful of podcasters gross over $1 million per year on the network) and roughly $4.5 million for Stitcher with roughly 8 million registered users (≈$0.56 per user). David's wife Jenny was the one Stitcher listener their analytics detected — running joke on show.

> **F3** Podcast CPMs cited on the episode range from roughly $25–100 versus a roughly $14 average YouTube CPM in 2014 — a high-value audience in a tiny absolute spend pool where Midroll may represent the majority of industry ad dollars. Facebook Paper shutdown noted in episode follow-ups as contrast to lightweight social launches.

> **F4** The top 10 podcast publishers account for roughly 40% of industry listens per David — an ad network must represent NPR and This American Life tier inventory or the addressable market is irrelevant despite premium CPMs. Hosts compare Apple podcast dominance to Internet Explorer bundling on Windows PCs.

> **F5** Ben Gilbert graded Midroll D on the $50M price and Stitcher B on user economics; Midroll launched the Howl premium tier (original shows plus ad-free archives) after acquiring Stitcher, with VP Eric Diehn stating the two products would intersect. Scripps technology chops questioned versus Microsoft acquiring Skype in prior Acquired episodes.

## Mental Model · *Platform Default Beats Vertical Stack*

> **Components**
>
> Podcasting's value chain (hosting, ads, client, analytics) wants vertical consolidation but Apple ships a free default client with every iPhone — the IE-on-Windows dynamic. Midroll owns spoken-word ad relationships; Stitcher offers an installed base for ad injection if the product improves under Midroll management.
>
> **Application**
>
> Model Apple strategic indifference as a binary platform risk. Startups need exclusive content or superior dynamic ad measurement — not merely stitching the existing RSS stack. Ben's PSL exploration passed because the MVP required client-side measurement Apple would not provide.

## What Makes It Work

> Midroll's edge was relationships with largest indie podcasts and spoken-word ad sales expertise — people business not tech. Stitcher offered installed base for ad injection if product improved. Scripps cash deployed when broadcast cash flows declined. Combined thesis: Netflix-of-podcasts with Howl premium originals + ad-free archives. Weakness: TAM $35M cannot justify $50M Midroll; Apple platform risk; Stitcher engagement uncertain; Ben graded Midroll D, Stitcher B. Gruber criticized Stitcher re-hosting/compression; hosts agree opportunity is huge but execution was 'crappy.' Secret Apple meetings with big publishers on monetization add platform risk.

## Key Insights

> **1.** TAM too small for price.
>
> **Q** Why grade Midroll D?
>
> **A** $50M on ≈$1.5–2M revenue requires massive industry expansion Scripps may not catalyze — entire market only ≈$35M spend (≈2% YoY). Ben: '50 million for something that makes 2 million a year?' Midroll may represent majority of industry ad spend but absolute dollars tiny. Must believe Stitcher + Howl premium tier changes economics — hosts skeptical. Ben's Wall Street Journal citation put total podcast ad spend at roughly $35 million industry-wide.

> **2.** Stitcher cheap on users.
>
> **Q** Why Stitcher B?
>
> **A** ≈$4.5M for ≈8M registered users (≈$0.56/user) cheap if engagement fixable; pointing Midroll ads into client plausible small business even if category winner unlikely. Strategy couples ad sales funnel with frontend client — but iOS ships default Podcasts app. David notes acquiring bad product and betting on hope is rare successful pattern on Acquired. John Gruber criticized Stitcher re-hosting audio and compressing files — hosts agree execution was poor.

> **3.** Apple is sleeping giant.
>
> **Q** Platform risk?
>
> **A** Apple created podcasting via iTunes directory, ships default app, ignores monetization — any API/reporting change could obsolete third-party stacks. Ben: 'Apple has just accidentally had this nascent, huge opportunity.' Secret meetings with big publishers on top-to-bottom monetization. Facebook-ization fear from Gruber and Ben Thompson on Stratechery/Exponent. Apple holds secret meetings with big publishers on top-to-bottom podcast monetization per Ben.

> **4.** Power law inventory.
>
> **Q** Who matters?
>
> **A** Top 10 publishers ≈40% listens — ad network must represent NPR/This American Life tier or TAM irrelevant. Midroll represents many largest podcasts; handful gross >$1M/year with ≈30% take. Manual sales minimums and Midroll contact requirement vs lightweight Facebook/Instagram ad buys — friction limits market growth. Midroll may represent the majority of podcast ad dollars despite the tiny absolute TAM.

> **5.** Consolidation inevitable but winner unclear.
>
> **Q** PSL lesson?
>
> **A** Ben explored dynamic ad insertion startup — needs client-side measurement Apple won't provide; heavy MVP vs Facebook/Instagram lightweight launches. Acast, Megaphone emerging 2015–16. Howl premium tier offers original shows and ad-free archives — Eric Diehn (Midroll VP BD) said Stitcher and Howl 'will intersect.' Hosts grade separately: Midroll D on price, Stitcher B on user economics. Eric Diehn said Howl premium originals and Stitcher client would intersect under Scripps ownership.
