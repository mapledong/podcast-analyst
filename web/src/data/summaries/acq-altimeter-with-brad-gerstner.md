## Key Facts

> **F2** Altimeter led Snowflake's Series B at approximately $175 million valuation; Snowflake IPO'd near $4.4 billion and traded around $100 billion market cap at episode recording.

> **F3** Snowflake remained over half of Altimeter's disclosed public portfolio at end of prior quarter — extreme concentration rare for hedge funds.

> **F4** Altimeter operates with roughly 30 people versus thousands at traditional venture firms — Brad explicitly does not compete with Sequoia at seed stage.

> **F5** RateSpecial/Altimeter heritage includes Zillow Series B lead and Booking/Expedia consumer-internet traffic arbitrage thesis spanning two decades.

## Mental Model · *Lifecycle Crossover Capital*

> **Components**
>
> Combine hedge-fund liquidity/market timing with venture privates access — dedicated pools for dry powder vs public book. Win by concentration (Snowflake, MongoDB) not median-of-VC indexing. Capital markets product (Roblox, Plaid) as differentiator vs early-stage brand. Secondaries and hold-through-IPO replace 'check hat at door' IPO dynamics.
>
> **Application**
>
> Later-stage founders evaluating crossover funds should weigh cap-table concentration and public-markets literacy vs brand halo of traditional VC. LPs: crossover works when privates are small slice of book with dedicated capital — not when public managers dabble without structure.

## What Makes It Work

> Altimeter's edge is lifecycle ownership — leading growth rounds pre-IPO and holding multi-bagger public positions (Snowflake ≈57× from ≈$175M Series B to ≈$100B) while offering capital-markets advisory competitors lack. Brad's operator/investor arc (IAC, Zillow board, Expedia deal) builds founder trust; ≈30-person firm avoids seed-stage dilution of attention. Ben notes Snowflake's Series B check was ≈$10–15M — hedge funds were not yet writing $50–100M growth rounds when Altimeter led. David cites Amazon sub-$500M at IPO as proof public markets can still capture multi-decade upside when crossover funds hold. Weaknesses: concentration risk (Snowflake dominance), market-timing reputation from 2008 launch, crossover copycats (Tiger, Coatue, Sequoia permanence). SPAC era association requires rehabilitation. Versus Sequoia/a16z: Altimeter won't win seed — competes at growth/public boundary where hedge funds historically did not lead Series Bs. Brand transfer to founders matters less than liquidity and cap-table management at IPO. Invest America policy advocacy extends brand beyond returns — political capital separate from investment edge. Booking/Expedia traffic thesis (Google as demand origination) predates cloud pillar — multi-trillion TAM framing recurs across pillars.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Crossover was not an obvious career after selling to Expedia.
>
> **Q** Why start a hedge fund in 2008?
>
> **A** Brad expected politics; Barry Diller/IAC and Paul Grewal's fund redirected him. Launching with ≈$3M at crisis bottom — family bankruptcy history made risk tolerance paradoxically high. Privates in hedge portfolios rose post-2008 as LPs sought growth exposure without classic VC fund structure.

> **2.** Snowflake conviction defined Altimeter's public-markets identity.
>
> **Q** Why lead a 'non-tech' Series B?
>
> **A** 2012 cloud data warehouse skeptics missed security/compliance shift — on-prem hacks vs AWS/Azure trust. ≈$175M entry vs ≈$100B later shows lifecycle hold beats quick flip. Over half public book in one name is feature (conviction) and bug (concentration).

> **3.** Altimeter competes on cap-table services not seed brand.
>
> **Q** How win deals vs Sequoia?
>
> **A** Founders cite concentrated capital, secondaries, IPO/direct-listing literacy — Dimitri/Modern Treasury pattern. Brad: Benchmark mantra at later stage; median VC indexing is bad strategy. ≈30 people vs thousands — different game.

> **4.** Venture industry may consolidate like LBO did.
>
> **Q** 3–5 year A+ vs C- scenario?
>
> **A** Brad sees LBO fragmentation → Blackstone/KKR consolidation parallel — niche strategies survive but scale players dominate. Altimeter A+ = continue lifecycle wins + policy (Invest America). C- = miss next Snowflake while competitors copy crossover playbook.

> **5.** Public markets can still capture late-stage growth.
>
> **Q** Does stay-private-longer kill crossover?
>
> **A** Amazon sub-$500M at IPO cited — public can still work. Go-public-early with Altimeter/Benchmark still on cap table differs from old 'check hat at door.' Founders get liquidity + brand retention simultaneously.
