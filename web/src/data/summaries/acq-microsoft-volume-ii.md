## Key Facts

> **F1** Netscape Aug 9, 1995 IPO ≈$3B cap; 1M to 15M users in one year. Microsoft licensed Mosaic from Spyglass for $2M — became Internet Explorer codebase.

> **F2** Dec 7, 1995 Bill announces IE free bundled in Windows 95 — Netscape stock ~-33% same day, never recovered; IE eventually >90% share by Nov 2001 settlement.

> **F3** Nov 1999 Jackson monopoly finding; June 7, 2000 breakup order — MSFT cap ≈$600B to ≈$270B (≈55%) over 12 months. Settlement Nov 2, 2001.

> **F4** Apple Aug 1997: $150M for ≈8% of ≈$2B Apple; IE default on Mac. By 2007: 40% of ≈$51B revenue from multi-year EAs; another 15% single-year EAs; 30% OEM Windows; 15% everything else.

> **F5** 2008 Yahoo bid reached $47B (15% search share; Alibaba 40% + Yahoo Japan worth >$30B). Oct 2007: $240M for 1.6% of Facebook ($15B valuation). Azure skunkworks ≈2006; Satya CEO Feb 4, 2014.

## Mental Model · *Embrace, Extend, Bundle*

> **Components**
>
> Detect exponential platforms early (Sinofsky 1994 Cornell snowstorm, Allard Jan 1994 memo), then bundle free on owned distribution (>90% Windows share). Spyglass $2M Mosaic license became IE; Dec 7 1995 bundling crashed Netscape ≈33%. Enterprise EA compounded: 55% of FY2007 ≈$51B revenue from multi-year agreements plus 15% single-year EAs. Same playbook failed in mobile/search where Microsoft lacked owned channels. Ballmer tripled profits ≈3x but inherited ≈80x P/E — multiple compression not operational failure. Azure (≈2006) and Xbox Live (40M users) incubated outside cash-cow divisions.
>
> **Application**
>
> Platform owners win when distribution competes with you — iOS/App Store lesson today. Separate consumer narrative losses from enterprise/cloud wins when grading CEOs. Incumbent disruption requires structural separation from profit centers (Server & Tools would have killed Azure). Yahoo $47B bid math: ≈$15B net for 15% search share versus Google at $300B+ revenue looked cheap in hindsight.

## What Makes It Work

> 1995–2000 Microsoft owned the PC OEM channel at >90% Windows share — an insurmountable distribution advantage for Netscape, which reached 15M users in one year but sold browsers as a loss leader for a platform dream. Microsoft licensed Spyglass Mosaic for $2M, bundled Internet Explorer free December 7, 1995 (Netscape stock fell ≈33% same day, never recovered), and closed AOL and Apple distribution loops by 1997. Enterprise switching costs compounded into the Ballmer era: by fiscal 2007, 40% of ≈$51B revenue flowed from multi-year enterprise agreements covering Windows, Office, and Exchange; another 15% from single-year EAs; only 30% from OEM Windows and 15% from everything else. Lotus Notes, acquired by IBM for $3.5B in 1995, was displaced as email became the enterprise killer app. The $47B Yahoo bid (2008) looked rational backing out Alibaba's 40% stake and Yahoo Japan worth >$30B — net ≈$15B for 15% search share against a Google generating $300B+ annual revenue by 2023. October 2007's $240M for 1.6% of Facebook ($15B valuation) bought banner-ad rights Microsoft later valued at 7×. Weaknesses: missed mobile (iPhone 2007; Mac still only ≈8% share), search (Bing launched 2009 vs Google 1998), and social (passed on Facebook acquisition). Vista/Longhorn execution failures and stack-ranking culture damaged morale. Gates deposition tapes — arguing over 'definition of definition' — halved market cap alongside antitrust ruling (≈$600B to ≈$270B). Public hated Microsoft while CIOs kept buying EAs. Antitrust settlement November 2001 never split the company; IE held ≈90% share. Azure incubation outside Server & Tools (≈2006) and Satya's 2014 CEO transition unlocked narrative without rebuilding enterprise fundamentals Ballmer tripled. Longhorn/Vista failure damaged developer trust while Mac gained mindshare despite ≈2–3% unit share in 2007. Bing launched 2009 — a decade after Google incorporated. Nokia ≈$7.5B acquisition opposed by Satya; write-down followed. Stack-ranking performance system created internal warfare. Yet CIOs renewing three-year EAs covering Windows, Office, and Exchange never stopped — the enterprise moat funded cloud R&D that consumer press ignored. Gates Internet tidal wave offsite after 1994 Cornell snowstorm memo changed company religion permanently.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Netscape lost when distribution competed.
>
> **Q** Why did free IE win?
>
> **A** Windows >90% share; IE bundled Dec 1995 free after $2M Spyglass license. Netscape IPO'd Aug 9 at ≈$3B, stock tripled to ≈$10B by Nov 1995, then crashed ≈33% Dec 7. Browser was loss leader for Netscape's platform dream — when AOL 1996 and Apple 1997 defaulted to IE, air supply cut. Netscape stock tripled to ≈$10B by Nov 1995 before December 7 IE bundling crashed it ≈33%.

> **2.** Breakup order was real then forgotten.
>
> **Q** Was Microsoft split?
>
> **A** Judge Jackson June 2000 ordered OS/apps separation — Ballmer and Gates assigned different companies. Cap ≈$600B to ≈$270B (≈55%) Nov 1999–2000. Jackson removed for press meetings; Nov 2, 2001 settlement. IE had ≈90% share at settlement. Fifteen months world believed breakup imminent. Judge Jackson removed for secret press meetings; settlement proposed Nov 2, 2001 with IE at ≈90% share.

> **3.** Ballmer era tripled business, not failed.
>
> **Q** Why flat stock?
>
> **A** Inherited ≈80x P/E at bubble peak; profits ≈3x 2000–2014 but multiple compressed. FY2007: 55% revenue from enterprise EAs, 30% OEM Windows, 15% other. Consumer narrative (Zune, Bing, Phone) drowned Azure truth. Unfair to grade like buying at all-time-high multiple. FY2007: only 15% of revenue was non-Windows/non-enterprise — consumer experiments were tiny slice of actual business.

> **4.** Azure required outside incubation.
>
> **Q** Why not Server & Tools?
>
> **A** Ray Ozzie ≈2006 team separate from Windows Server cash cow. Xbox Live had 40M users; search at scale built distributed-computing DNA. Satya opposed Nokia ≈$7.5B; became CEO Feb 2014. Same pattern needed for mobile — arrived too late. Xbox Live's 40M users and search infrastructure built distributed-computing DNA for Azure team.

> **5.** Gates deposition was PR disaster.
>
> **Q** Why did tapes matter?
>
> **A** Bill argued over 'definition of definition' — video leaked beyond transcript. Boies played clips shaping judge/press perception. Cap halved during antitrust fight while dot-com bubble burst. Hardened Microsoft aggression as consumer taste shifted to Google/Apple aesthetics. Yahoo eventually sold for ≈$5B to Verizon/Apollo — counterfactual Microsoft ownership might not have saved search share.
