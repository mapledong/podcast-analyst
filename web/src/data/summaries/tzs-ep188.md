## Background

> Practical Investments ep.188 (June 21, 2026) is a Wu Xiaobo Channel live replay where Yongqing teaches how to do deep investment research — process, pitfalls, and information sources. He frames good investments as self-knowledge plus business plus market understanding, then walks a full buy-side SOP: idea sourcing (prior coverage, bottleneck chains, 13F opereye, peers), fast screening, deep coverage modules (industry history, unit economics, management, rivals, financials, market/valuation), field research, and ongoing tracking. Emphasis: rule-based decisions beat lottery buys on one headline quarter; AI helps 70–80% of prep if inputs are clean.

---

## Key Facts

> **F1** Three-lens framework: money attributes (3+ year capital, ≈10–15% target), business quality (three value styles — growth, quality-at-fair-price, deep value — session focuses on quality), and market context (sector rotation, valuation regimes). Good research SOP: idea → screen → deep cover → verify via research → track anomalies — like clearing a game map before fighting.

> **F2** Deep-cover checklist: industry/company history, business and unit economics, management and cap structure, competitor count (prefer ≤2–3 strong players), financial forensics, market narrative and valuation, then distill to 2–3 tracking questions. Quality single-name work ≈1.5M characters of public material plus expert interviews; skip names with fraud flags, chronic decline, or serial pivoting.

> **F3** Information hygiene: filter PR podcasts, require buyer-side voices with skin in the game, cross-check via industry veterans and investor 360° (long, watchlist, sellers, shorts). AI bottleneck chain example — GPU → optics → HBM storage → CPU → advanced packaging — shows simple vs hard problems; ≈30 of 80 tracked US managers bought TSM in Q1 as a 'simple body' signal.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Process beats lottery even when luck pays once.
>
> **Q** Why is buying on one great quarter dangerous?
>
> **A** Yongqing says a single strong print inside no prior map is still gambling — you may win but the workflow is wrong. Institutions log idea source, screen rationale, and cover conclusions so luck does not scale into oversized adds after +30% runs.

> **2.** Simple bodies first; hard problems optional.
>
> **Q** How should retail handle AI semis?
>
> **A** In chip cycles, NVDA/ASML/TSM were 'simple' before storage became the hard HBM bottleneck. Master one or two names with full SOP — ≈10× PE, ≈10% growth, ≈5% yield ideal — rather than twenty 5% bets you do not understand.

> **3.** AI is research staff, not research substitute.
>
> **Q** Where does AI fit the SOP?
>
> **A** Models hit ≈75–85% on financial frameworks and reading plans if you feed primary sources — not PR. Build curated info libraries, simulate great investors' question sets, then use humans for field checks and edge identification.
