## Background

> Dan Sundheim founded D1 after a long run at Viking, where by 2016 he managed just over half the firm's capital. D1 invests across public and private markets and owns or has owned stakes in SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic, Ramp, Rivian, and many public companies. The conversation is partly an AI market map and partly a biography of an investor: early Value Investors Club work, discovering an accounting fraud in Orthodontic Centers of America, the GameStop drawdown, a June 2022 LP dinner at the trough, and the importance of passion, loyalty, and leadership in stock picking.

---

## Key Facts

> **F1** D1 first invested in OpenAI at the $125B round. Sundheim says 4 or 5 LLMs probably matter long term because capital needs are too high for many new entrants; Anthropic was initially compared by skeptics to Lyft versus Uber, but its enterprise and coding focus created strong traction. He compares LLM economics to Netflix's upfront content spend plus Spotify's personalization stickiness — real businesses, but with unprecedented capital intensity. Gross margins do not look commoditized, but returns on capital and adoption speed are the real debate.

> **F2** By 2016 Sundheim was managing just over half of Viking's capital — about 55% — after joining in 2002 as an analyst with no portfolio. He left to start D1 in 2018 at age 40, recognizing Viking would not let one person manage more than half the firm's capital indefinitely and that he still had the energy for a new fund. He had gone from no portfolio to CIO while covering banks, healthcare, industrials, and TMT.

> **F3** GameStop and the 2021–2022 drawdown forced portfolio construction changes. D1 held semiannual LP dinners over 4 nights in early June 2022 right after the May drawdown trough; president Jeremy initially wanted to cancel, but Sundheim insisted they meet investors and explain a singles-and-doubles approach with less risk. Stock selection stayed the same, but the book would be much less risk-prone going forward.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Private AI investing now informs public market investing more than ever.
>
> **Q** Why own OpenAI, Anthropic, and SpaceX?
>
> **A** Because AI will affect almost every public company. Seeing private leaders up close gives D1 a view on model progress, adoption, capex, software risk, and real economy second-order effects — from OpenAI consumer traction to Anthropic enterprise coding wins.

> **2.** Hyperscalers can grow faster and still become worse businesses.
>
> **Q** How can AWS/Azure/GCP be worse if AI demand is huge?
>
> **A** Their customer base shifts from every company to a few giant LLMs. Those LLMs may insource compute once they have free cash flow, and GPU clusters/inference look different from traditional CPU cloud workloads.

> **3.** Software is wounded, not dead.
>
> **Q** Does coding AI kill software companies?
>
> **A** Sundheim expects a worse model but not instant death for systems of record. ERP and CRM are hard to vibe-code for the whole enterprise, but incumbents must integrate AI like Walmart integrated e-commerce.
