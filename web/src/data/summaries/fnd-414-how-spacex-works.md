## Key Insights

> **1.** Materials are not the cost — process is.
>
> **Q** What is the 2% insight?
>
> **A** Aerospace-grade metals are ≈2% of rocket price vs ≈30% for cars — supplier tiers stack 15% margins. SpaceX builds in-house, uses bike shocks on Dragon, and runs Merlin engines in million-unit production mindset versus $25M Russian RD-180 imports.

> **2.** Standardize like Model T, not bespoke NASA.
>
> **Q** How does platform strategy work?
>
> **A** Shared Falcon diameter, nine-engine cores, published User's Guide with bolt circles — customers adapt to SpaceX, not vice versa. ≈40 identical Falcon 9s annually enable automotive learning curves impossible on hand-built Atlas variants.

> **3.** Development blows up; operations must not.
>
> **Q** How reconcile explosions with crew safety?
>
> **A** Starship test flights intentionally push until failure — 'unscheduled disassemblies' feed telemetry; crewed Dragon runs conservative margins. Cheaper prototypes enable more iterations; Musk: any tech needs many iterations — blowing up is data.
