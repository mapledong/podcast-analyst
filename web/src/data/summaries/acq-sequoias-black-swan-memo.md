## Mental Model · *Defense Then Offense (Sequoia Crisis)*

> **Components**
>
> Black swan = sudden 10X change in fundamentals (Grove parallel). Phase 1: email CEOs, cut burn, extend runway — Sequoia feared inciting panic but chose transparency. Phase 2: hunt dislocations — new needs (telehealth, delivery) and business-model upgrades (PayPal: revenue only after crash forced value proof). China early signal through portfolio gave conviction before U.S. woke up. Remote war room = partner cadence on portfolio, not travel.
>
> **Application**
>
> Founders should assume revenue step-change down and fix unit economics before chasing growth. Investors separate defense actions from offense bets — same week, different budgets. LPs: denominator effect matters more than headline dry powder. Great companies (Square) survive; public mispricing ≠ fundamental failure.

## Key Insights

> **1.** China visibility preceded U.S. denial.
>
> **Q** Why March 5?
>
> **A** Sequoia's China portfolio showed severity weeks earlier; U.S. still normal. Roelof weighed panic risk vs responsibility — chose early warning. NBA shutdown six days later validated timing; memo aged like '20 years in 20 days.'

> **2.** PayPal proves business-model pivots beat cost cuts alone.
>
> **Q** What did PayPal do post-2000?
>
> **A** Roelof (PayPal CFO era): no viable model until after crash — necessity forced charging for real value. Highest-leverage startup change is business model, not just S&M trim. Buffett/Marks tide-goes-out quote applied to COVID revenue tests.

> **3.** War room went virtual.
>
> **Q** How did Sequoia operate March 2020?
>
> **A** Doug Leone's 1999 physical war room replaced by remote partner cadence focused on portfolio triage — what they stopped doing (travel, new processes) mattered as much as CEO outreach. Hiring bar and remote-first bets (Zapier) shifted.

> **4.** Dry powder ≠ deployment speed.
>
> **Q** Will funding continue at bull pace?
>
> **A** Record VC commitments exist but LP private allocation caps (≈15% target vs 40% stuck) may slow new fund closes and shift mix toward follow-ons vs new seeds — too early to tell but denominator effect scary for allocators.

> **5.** Do not liquidate quality in panic.
>
> **Q** Why Square?
>
> **A** Square IPO misjudged even in bull market; Roelof stayed on board. Acquired pre/post IPO episodes show great companies hunker down — memo's closing note: survive, don't sell into dislocation.
