## Mental Model · *Resilience + Optionality Barbell*

> **Components**
>
> Resilience: durable compounding with governors (negative feedback loops) — TSMC's steady ≈20% for 50 years beats Groupon hypergrowth. Prefer companies leaving consumer/partner surplus (Porter: long-term shareholder maximization via restraint). Optionality: small positions where payoff is multiverse-outcome dependent (3–7 sigma events create most economic value) — document thesis to rotate out when invalid. Avoid prediction in unprecedented multiples environment (2021) — own railroads/semis across multiverses where demand persists. Ant colony: resilience without over-optimization/productivity theater. Barbell: ≈60% resilience sleeve, ≈40% optionality per episode discussion.
>
> **Application**
>
> Public portfolios: don't conflate moats with resilience — extraction maximalists (Apple cited) fragile to regulation/sentiment. Size optionality for power law (40% sleeve, many bets). Trim winners when volatility spikes (Zoom/Peloton lesson). CEOs should run core for resilience while funding optional Bets (Amazon playbook). Semi research: watch TSMC OIP ecosystem, don't assume ASML vertical integration — layer-specific value capture. Brinton's ultramarathon (24+ hours) is where hosts say best ideas emerge — complexity mindset favors patience over quarterly prediction. Capital Camp meet (Patrick O'Shaughnessy/Brent Beshore) bridged Knowledge Project interview to this Acquired special after TSMC episode used NZS among 65 sources.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Moats ≠ resilience — extraction kills durability.
>
> **Q** Why don't NZS investors chase wide-moat Apple?
>
> **A** Apple's value capture leaves less consumer surplus — resilient companies deliberately leave money on table for customers/partners (Porter stakeholder argument, Ho Nam/Altos parallel). Moats can indicate strength but also fragility when regulation, sentiment, or paradigm shifts hit maximal extractors. TSMC/TI exemplify slow governed growth with partner ecosystems (Open Innovation Platform). NZS white paper frames ant-colony resilience versus productivity theater.

> **2.** Optionality requires cultural permission for low conviction.
>
> **Q** How do you reward "I don't know" in a partnership?
>
> **A** Memos documenting why optionality bet could work — rotate out when thesis breaks. Coin-flip +EV game (+50%/-40% per flip) still wipes most players; need ≈40% portfolio in asymmetric bets (eBay/PayPal model: resilient marketplace + PayPal call). VC wisdom "ride winners" rejected — Zoom/Peloton soared then retraced; trim on volatility. Guardrails against false precision in bull market (2021 unprecedented multiples). Culture must reward "I don't know but it could be huge" without forcing conviction theater.

> **3.** Compounding math favors marathon growth rates.
>
> **Q** Why prefer 15% for 15 years over hypergrowth?
>
> **A** White paper table: 15%×10 ≈ 300% return; extend five more years at 15% nearly doubles prior decade outcome. Hypergrowth often lacks negative feedback governors (Uber-style) — TSMC ≈20% for 50 years destroys short bursts. Negative feedback = natural cap enabling decades. Derivative flatness beats spike-crash even if headline CAGR lower early. NZS trims winners on volatility — Zoom/Peloton retraced after multiverse wins played out. Warren/Charlie "laziness bordering on sloth" cited as feature.

> **4.** Semis are multiverse-critical infrastructure — but layered.
>
> **Q** TSMC vs Samsung vs ASML — who has what power?
>
> **A** TSMC pure-play foundry wins partner trust (no customer conflict); Samsung integrated (memory in iPhone, competes with clients) — hosts oversimplified in TSMC ep. ASML monopoly on EUV tools but cannot operate fabs — services + onsite assembly at TSMC ≠ becoming TSMC. Moore's law "spirit" continues via packaging/chiplets when lithography slows. Tesla charging network (MKBHD road trip) shows integrated UX moat analog.

> **5.** Geopolitical risk on TSMC is known — not black swan hedgeable easily.
>
> **Q** What hedges Taiwan sovereignty tail risk?
>
> **A** NZS holds TSMC as resilience bet anyway — "10/10 multiverses need semis/railroads." China ADR risk acknowledged; no perfect hedge if US-China conflict — optionality sleeve elsewhere. Compare to stop-sign airbags: useless until catastrophic moment. Research via obscure conference YouTube (Gwynne Shotwell talks) mirrors Acquired method — management access useful at their AUM. Brinton ultramarathon (24+ hours) is where hosts say best portfolio ideas emerge.
