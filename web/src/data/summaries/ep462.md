## Background

> Shyam Sankar is Palantir's CTO and a key architect of forward deployed engineering. He uses military 'heretics' like Hyman Rickover, Billy Mitchell, John Boyd, Andrew Higgins, Kelly Johnson, and Bill Knudsen as a lens for founders who fight bureaucracy to build what wins. The episode explains Palantir as an enterprise operating system for decisions, built around ontology and deployed in the field, then widens into American reindustrialization, China, defense procurement, cost-plus contracting, rare earths, generic drugs, AI, and why cultures need rebellion to survive scale.

---

## Key Facts

> **F1** Before the Berlin Wall fell, only about 6% of major weapon-system spending went to defense specialists; today Sankar says it is 86%, with 94% once going to dual-purpose companies like Chrysler building missiles and minivans. The 1993 Pentagon 'Last Supper' told 15 of 51 primes to consolidate; the industry went from 51 primes down to 5 as the post–Cold War defense budget was slashed. China mapped US military technology dependencies after Gulf War I.

> **F2** Rickover built the first nuclear submarine in 7 years; US nuclear submarines have had no deaths from nuclear incidents, unlike Soviet crews who spent months recovering from radiation exposure. Sankar says AI is David's slingshot: if the American worker becomes 50x more productive, the frontier of what can be made domestically moves. Co-locating R&D and production and iterating factories like software become critical.

> **F3** Apple spent the inflation-adjusted equivalent of about 2.5 Marshall Plans building talent and capacity in China; Sankar asks why the US cannot spend 1 Marshall Plan at home. On Palantir delivery, customer value that took about 8 weeks 2 years ago now feels like roughly 1 week as enterprise autonomy deployments compound. Anduril fits his heretic model by selling working capability instead of cost-plus procurement.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Forward deployed engineering is product discovery through operational truth, not fancy sales engineering.
>
> **Q** What does Palantir actually do?
>
> **A** It builds an enterprise operating system for decisions. The ontology maps how the institution actually thinks and acts, making the business programmable across decisions like quality, production planning, and in-service support — the layer where AIP now compresses delivery from 8 weeks toward 1.

> **2.** America's defense problem is cultural and industrial, not just budgetary.
>
> **Q** Why did deterrence weaken?
>
> **A** The US moved from an American industrial base full of dual-purpose companies and founder figures to a financialized defense specialist base. We make exquisite things in small numbers while China is best at mass production.

> **3.** AI is valuable because it can change the economics of reindustrialization.
>
> **Q** How can the US make things again?
>
> **A** Sankar says AI is David's slingshot: if the American worker becomes 50x more productive, the frontier of what can be made domestically moves. Co-locating R&D and production and iterating factories like software become critical.
