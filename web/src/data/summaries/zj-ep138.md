## Key Insights

> **1.** Post-training is the era; pre-training is the foundation.
>
> **Q** What changes in Luo's framework?
>
> **A** Base models supply worldview and knowledge; post-training supplies agency — tool use, coding, overnight tasks. Without pre-train priors, RLHF and SFT chase shallow behaviors. GPU ≈3:1:1 split reflects rising post-train spend as products ship, not just lab papers.

> **2.** OpenClaw moment is interaction shock, like ChatGPT.
>
> **Q** Why emphasize 2am–6am first use?
>
> **A** Breakthrough felt in autonomous overnight work, not benchmark release — same pattern Su Yu draws for language agents. Product moments lag papers; investors should track dogfood hours and task completion, not parameter counts.

> **3.** Self-evolving agents remain TBD amid framework clubs.
>
> **Q** Where is venture edge?
>
> **A** Dozens of agent frameworks resemble social clubs — orchestration without compounding learning. True self-evolution needs closed feedback loops (coding fixes, formal proofs). Until solved, post-train teams with flat orgs and real workloads win over wrapper startups.
