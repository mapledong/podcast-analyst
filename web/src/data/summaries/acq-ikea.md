## Key Insights

> **1.** Flat-pack was logistics insight, not design fad.
>
> **Q** Why remove table legs?
>
> **A** 1956 Gillis Lundgren couldn't fit table in car — removed legs, realized storage efficiency. Revenue ≈6M kronor (1955) to ≈40M kronor (1961). Competitors mail-ordering assembled furniture paid breakage and freight — IKEA passed savings as lowest price. POÄNG chair: ≈30M sold since 1976. Kamprad's dyslexia drove Swedish place names instead of SKUs — memorable branding accident. Flat-pack became default across entire range by late 1950s after LÖVET insight.

> **2.** Showroom without takeaway was intentional.
>
> **Q** Why not sell in store initially?
>
> **A** 1953 Älmhult exhibition let customers touch catalog goods before mail ordering. Kamprad: 'Use catalog to tempt people to exhibition.' Meatballs from day one (≈1B/year today) increased dwell. Second store cost 17M kronor (≈$3M) on ≈100M krona revenue — big bet they could afford. Second store cost 17M kronor (≈$3M) on ≈100M krona revenue — large bet affordable from cash flows only. Original Älmhult location bought for 13,000 kronor versus 17M kronor second store investment.

> **3.** Supplier boycott forced vertical design.
>
> **Q** Why own product development?
>
> **A** Swedish furniture makers blocked IKEA from trade fairs (1960s). Ingvar designed in-house, sourced Poland — board-on-frame LACK at ≈$9.99. Breathtaking price products mandated 50%+ below substitutes. Adversity created moat catalog resellers couldn't match. Ingka Foundation disburses €200–300M/year charitably while operating stores via 3% royalty to Inter IKEA. Board-on-frame particle sandwich from Poland enabled LACK ≈$9.99 US pricing in 2024.

> **4.** 500 kronor is the only outside capital.
>
> **Q** How did IKEA scale without investors?
>
> **A** Positive cash flow from matchboxes → pens → furniture reinvested over 81 years. Kamprad owned 100% until foundation; €25B cash today. Revenue path: ≈$20M (mid-60s) → $2B (1980s) → $10B (1990s) → $20B (2007) — pure compounding. Kamprad net worth reached ≈$60B; Sweden abolished wealth/inheritance tax mid-2000s. Kamprad moved back to Småland late in life after Sweden abolished wealth taxes.

> **5.** Germany scale beats home market.
>
> **Q** Where is IKEA largest?
>
> **A** Germany — not Sweden — proving Småland price obsession exported to urbanizing Europe. 476 stores serve ≈700M customers globally. Japan entry 1975 failed after 12 years; China 1998 became huge market. Urban apartments needed affordable movable furniture. Japan failed 12 years (1975–1986); China entry 1998 became major growth market. 700M customers across 476 stores implies massive repeat traffic per location annually.
