## Background

> Jesse Pooji breaks down CrowdStrike with Roniel Desai, a senior public market investor focused on enterprise software. Desai explains how George Kurtz and Dmitri Alperovitch left McAfee in 2011 to build cloud-native endpoint security: agents on every laptop, phone, and server feed a centralized threat graph where machine learning spots behavioral anomalies across apps and geographies.
>
> Desai contrasts static McAfee firewalls with CrowdStrike's next-gen antivirus plus EDR, walks the 2016 DNC Russian-hacker attribution, Sony and healthcare breach work that built the incident-response brand, and how COVID remote work shattered perimeter-only security. He quantifies a market IDC revised from $10 billion to $18.5 billion by 2025 as customers upgrade from $1-per-endpoint legacy antivirus to $16–$22 monthly next-gen bundles, then maps module expansion from 10 at IPO to 22 today with Humio logging enabling XDR across partner data feeds.

## Key Facts

> **F1** As of August 2022 CrowdStrike traded near a $45 billion enterprise value on $1.9 billion ARR growing 61% year-over-year (down from 72% a year prior), with ≈18,000 customers growing ≈60% year-over-year; top 100 customers averaged $2 million+ each, top 25 averaged $4.5 million, and 43% of revenue came from accounts spending more than $1 million annually — making it the eighth-largest cloud-native SaaS vendor by ARR and, Desai argues, the fastest grower at this scale besides Snowflake.

> **F2** IDC's endpoint-security TAM estimate moved from $10 billion (June 2020) to $13 billion (June 2021) to $18.5 billion (2022) as customers layered EDR, managed detection, and cloud-server agents atop legacy antivirus; legacy vendors Symantec, McAfee, and Trend Micro still held ≈60% of spend while charging roughly $1 per endpoint monthly versus CrowdStrike's ≈$16 next-gen AV plus ≈$6 EDR (≈$22 list before discounts).

> **F3** CrowdStrike expanded from 10 modules at its 2019 IPO to 22 by 2022: 70% of customers now buy more than three modules (versus 30% in 2017 and 50% in 2019), averaging almost five modules overall and seven among million-dollar accounts; non-core modules already exceed 20% of ARR and grow twice as fast, including a $80 million identity acquisition that scaled from $6 million to $50 million ARR growing 30% quarter-over-quarter and Humio-powered XDR ingesting partner feeds from Cloudflare, Okta, and others.

## Mental Model · *Casino Security vs. Airport TSA*

> **Components**
>
> Legacy endpoint products act like TSA: block known-bad payloads at the perimeter, then stop watching once traffic passes. CrowdStrike's agent monitors inter-app behavior, geolocation, and cross-endpoint flows in a shared threat graph — more like casino surveillance that follows betting patterns after entry. COVID destroyed the office perimeter model where VPN hairpins forced all remote traffic through corporate firewalls; protecting distributed laptops became mandatory. A single lightweight agent upgrades over the air, hosts multiple modules, and lets CrowdStrike remediate automatically — turning off a compromised device without needing email-server control.
>
> **Application**
>
> Desai says architectural bets beat being first: CrowdStrike was third next-gen endpoint vendor to GA but won on scalable one-agent architecture plus managed services customers actually wanted to buy. Channel VARs and MSSPs create a flywheel — partners push CrowdStrike even at lower wholesale margins because 20% higher ASPs, easier installs, and cross-sell still earn more than legacy vendors. Investors should model TAM expansion (not static IDC share caps), platform module attach, and whether open XDR beats Microsoft's closed bundle — Desai estimates ≈90 cents to acquire $1 of ARR at ≈30% incremental margins implying ≈40% incremental ROIC.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Threat graph beats per-device blocklists.
>
> **Q** How is CrowdStrike different from McAfee?
>
> **A** Agents collect app-to-app and internet traffic from every endpoint into one threat graph where ML flags anomalies — a California user receiving packets from Russia, or ESPN asking for a saved password mid-session. McAfee firewalls only stop known malware at ingress; they cannot see lateral movement after a phishing link grants access.

> **2.** COVID rewired security architecture.
>
> **Q** Why did remote work accelerate CrowdStrike?
>
> **A** Old IT assumed assets sat behind a powerful office firewall with VPN hairpins for remote access. When entire workforces left the building, hairpinning Salesforce and AWS traffic through capacity-constrained firewalls broke; the only fix was endpoint agents protecting home laptops that could otherwise become backdoors into corporate SaaS accounts.

> **3.** Platform modules compound on one agent.
>
> **Q** How does CrowdStrike expand beyond endpoint?
>
> **A** Humio logging plus partner XDR feeds unify endpoint, identity, and third-party security data; cloud workload protection drops the same agent onto spun-up AWS servers. Desai notes customers with $1M+ spend average seven modules while subscription gross margins stay near 77% because the underlying agent stays constant as attach rises.

## Investment Ideas

> **1. CRWD** · 🟢 LONG · ●●○ Medium
>
> Desai sees a rare growth compounder — 61% ARR growth at $1.9 billion scale, expanding TAM, channel flywheel, and platform module attach — but Microsoft Office E5 bundling and closed XDR platforms from Microsoft and Palo Alto are credible threats; valuation near $45 billion already prices flawless platform execution.

## Chronology

> *CrowdStrike*
>
> **1990s–2011** George Kurtz builds and sells a security startup to McAfee, becomes McAfee CTO, then leaves with Dmitri Alperovitch to found CrowdStrike in 2011.
>
> **2014** Sony hack: CrowdStrike deploys across 40,000 endpoints and attributes the breach to North Korea within a day.
>
> **2015–2016** Incident-response work on Chinese healthcare hacking and DNC Russian attribution puts CrowdStrike in national headlines.
>
> **2019** IPO with 10 modules; Cylance and Carbon Black acquired by legacy vendors while Broadcom acquires Symantec and sheds smaller customers.
>
> **2020** COVID remote work shatters perimeter-only security; CrowdStrike buys identity vendor for $80 million (≈$6 million ARR).
>
> **2021–2022** Humio acquisition enables XDR; modules grow to 22 with 70% of customers on 3+ products as ARR reaches $1.9 billion.
>
> **2022-09** Desai discusses CrowdStrike on Business Breakdowns EP.72 at ≈$45 billion enterprise value.
