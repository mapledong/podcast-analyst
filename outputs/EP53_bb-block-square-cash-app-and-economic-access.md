## Background

> Amish Corlett of TDM Growth Partners breaks down Block (formerly Square), founded in 2009 by Jack Dorsey and Jim McKelvie after a missed $2,000 card sale exposed how micro-merchants were locked out of payments. Corlett maps a $75 billion company—market cap comparable to Citigroup—with two main ecosystems: Square serving roughly 3 million merchants at $2.3 billion of gross profit, and Cash App reaching about 100 million annual active users. Gross profit grew from $500 million at the 2015 IPO to over $5 billion, a roughly 50% CAGR driven mostly organically. The conversation walks payments economics (3% merchant charge, just over 1% net take), Cash App monetization through instant deposit and Cash Card issuing, the Afterpay acquisition as connective tissue, Tidal and Bitcoin bets tied to the same mission, and competitive positioning versus banks, PayPal/Venmo, and Toast.

---

## Key Facts

> **F1** Block runs two scaled ecosystems: Square serves roughly 3 million merchants generating about $2.3 billion of gross profit on ≈$2.8 billion of net revenue, while Cash App has roughly 100 million annual active users—scale comparable to JPMorgan's 71 million retail cardholders and Bank of America's ≈40 million digital customers. IPO-era gross profit of $500 million has compounded to over $5 billion at roughly 50% CAGR, mostly organic.

> **F2** Unit economics are strong on both sides: Cash App customer payback stays under 12 months with 6x ROI over three years and 125% net gross profit dollar retention; Square merchants pay back in ≈18 months at 3x ROI. Cash App acquires users at ≈$10 versus hundreds of dollars for traditional banks—a 10x+ CAC gap—and Cash Card holders (30%+ of users) generate about 5x the gross profit of peer-to-peer-only users.

> **F3** Afterpay adds two-sided linkage: ≈20 million consumer actives and 100,000+ merchants saw 20–30% conversion lifts; it generated about 1 million merchant leads per day from 16 million actives, now exposed to 4x more Cash App users. Closed-loop Square–Cash App transactions lift Block's net take from just over 1% toward 3%, versus ≈1% for competitors like Toast on like-for-like payments; instant deposit supplies roughly half of Cash App gross profit.

## Mental Model · *Trust-First Dual Ecosystem With Closed-Loop Take Rates*

> **Components**
>
> Block's wedge is access: Square flipped incumbent 'verify first' onboarding into trust-first instant approval for 99% of merchants using data and AI fraud detection, then layered software (roughly 80% gross margin), loans (6% servicing fee with third-party balance-sheet partners), and hardware. Cash App started as a 2013 hack-week project targeting ≈60 million unbanked Americans, building peer-to-peer network effects and a culturally resonant brand. Card issuing—Cash Card at ≈30%+ adoption and Square Card for merchants—captures interchange that otherwise flows to Chase or Citi. When a Cash App user pays a Square seller in-network, economics that once leaked to external issuers and networks stay inside Block.
>
> **Application**
>
> Corlett argues the strategic prize is Mastercard/Amex-style two-sided network effects between consumers and merchants, accelerated by Afterpay's transaction-level data and merchant discovery (potentially 2+ billion leads per year on current footprint, implying $100–200 billion of GMV at $50–100 average order values). Square's ≈$200 billion seller GMV base plus Afterpay's demand generation could add multi-billion-dollar revenue. Risks concentrate on trust—merchants and consumers run daily operations through Block—and on balance-sheet-heavy products (Afterpay, Cash App Borrow, FDIC license) slowing the product culture that won against Amazon, Intuit, and PayPal in hardware payments.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Payments processing funds a software business, not the reverse.
>
> **Q** How should investors read Square's margin structure?
>
> **A** Corlett says Square charges ≈3% on a $100 transaction and keeps just over 1%, with the rest flowing to card issuers and networks. Blended Square segment gross margin (≈$2.3 billion on ≈$2.8 billion net revenue) looks like software because integrated POS, payroll, and lending attach multiply gross profit dollars per merchant versus standalone payments.

> **2.** Afterpay is discovery and network glue, not just BNPL.
>
> **Q** Why did Block pay ≈25% of its market cap in stock for Afterpay?
>
> **A** Beyond BNPL's 20–30% merchant conversion lift, Afterpay generated roughly 1 million leads per day to merchants. Embedding that discovery in Cash App quadruples the consumer base exposed to those leads. Corlett estimates a $100–200 billion GMV opportunity from that funnel on top of Square's ≈$200 billion seller volume.

> **3.** Cash App beat Venmo by brand and unit economics, not first-mover advantage.
>
> **Q** How does Block compete with PayPal?
>
> **A** Venmo led peer-to-peer early, but Cash App became the growth leader with sub-$10 acquisition costs, sub-12-month payback, and cultural marketing—from Cash App Fridays to hip-hop lyrics—that resonates with Gen Z. Corlett sees room for both, but Cash App still has MAU growth while Venmo shifts toward monetizing existing users.

## Investment Ideas

> **1. SQ** · 🟢 LONG · ●●○ Medium
>
> Corlett's bull case rests on two $2B+ gross-profit ecosystems compounding through product attach, closed-loop payments that triple net take on in-network volume, and Afterpay-driven merchant discovery—against a financial-services TAM still littered with legacy UX, with culture and trust as the main execution risks.

> **2. PYPL** · 🔴 SHORT · ●○○ Low
>
> PayPal retains a durable moat, but Cash App has overtaken Venmo in P2P user growth with far lower CAC (≈$10 vs. hundreds for banks) and stronger Gen Z brand penetration while Venmo pivots to monetizing existing users.

## Chronology

> *Block (Square)*
>
> **2009** Jack Dorsey and Jim McKelvie found Square after a missed $2,000 card sale; prototype card reader ships within a month.
>
> **2013** Cash App launches from a hack-week project led by Brian Grassadone, targeting unbanked and underbanked U.S. consumers.
>
> **Late 2015** Square IPOs with roughly $500 million of gross profit; organic expansion into loans, Cash Card, and integrated hardware-software POS follows.
>
> **2014-2021** Afterpay builds BNPL in Australia, enters the U.S. with marquee merchants, reaching ≈20 million consumer actives and 100,000+ merchants.
>
> **2021** Block acquires Afterpay in an all-stock deal issuing about 25% of market cap; buys Tidal to serve long-tail music artists as small businesses.
>
> **2021-2022** Company rebrands to Block; Jack Dorsey advances Bitcoin strategy via Spiral, TBD, corporate BTC holdings, and Cash App trading.
>
> **2022** Corlett breakdown on Business Breakdowns EP.53 maps $75 billion market cap, $5B+ gross profit, and the closed-loop vision.
