## Background

> 3i began as postwar industrial-finance infrastructure and became a listed private-equity company. Its modern transformation starts after the 2009 crisis, when a 9-for-7 rights issue raised roughly £700M and exposed the flaws of a loose investment model. Under current leadership from 2011, 3i narrowed focus and kept economics that would otherwise accrue to outside fund managers. The central asset is Action, a European discount retailer bought in 2011 when it had about €700M of sales; by 2023, sales were roughly €11B. Action now represents more than half of net asset value, making 3i less diversified than a typical PE firm but far more operating-company-like in its economics.
>
>  The strategic lesson is that 3i solved the listed-PE agency problem by owning more of the upside in fewer assets. The transcript contrasts a hypothetical external-manager model where investing £750M a year might produce £50M of carry-like economics with owning the balance-sheet return directly. That difference is why the Action stake changed 3i's identity: it became less like a fee manager and more like a concentrated holding company with private-equity skills.

## Key Facts

> **F1** 3i traces its roots to 1945 with about £15M of capital, shortened its name in 1983, and IPO'd in 1994 at around £1.5B of value.

> **F2** After trouble in the 2000s, 3i completed a highly dilutive 9-for-7 rights issue in 2009 that raised about £700M, then refocused under new leadership from 2011.

> **F3** Action was bought in 2011 at about €700M of sales and reached roughly €11B in 2023; it now represents north of 50% of 3i's NAV.


---

## Mental Model · *Listed PE as Concentrated Operating Compounder*

> **Components**
>
> Own permanent capital, avoid giving away upside through external fund economics, and concentrate behind the best asset. The tradeoff is higher idiosyncratic risk.
>
> **Application**
>
> TGOPY/3i investors are underwriting Action as much as a diversified manager. Returns can be exceptional if Action keeps scaling, but NAV sensitivity to one retailer is the key risk.

## Key Insights

> **1.** The model changed after pain.
>
> **Q** Why did 2009 matter?
>
> **A** The rights issue forced a rethink; the company stopped behaving like a broad asset collector and focused on fewer, higher-conviction investments.

> **2.** Action drives the story.
>
> **Q** Why is Action central?
>
> **A** It grew from about €700M of sales in 2011 to roughly €11B by 2023 and now exceeds half of NAV.

> **3.** Permanent capital changes incentives.
>
> **Q** What is different from a fund?
>
> **A** 3i keeps more upside on its own balance sheet rather than earning only management fees and carry on outside capital.
