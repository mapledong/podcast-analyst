## Mental Model · *Verified Work Becomes Automated Work*

> **Components**
>
> Google's TPU edge is real but may be temporary. If Google is the low-cost token producer, it can suck economic oxygen from the ecosystem by pricing aggressively. Baker thinks Blackwell training and then inference could remove that advantage and make negative 30% margins painful for Google. Blackwell is not just a chip; it is a system transition. The rack goes from air-cooled to liquid-cooled and from roughly 1,000 pounds to a much more complex deployment. Baker says it takes 6-9 months after deployment to work out bugs, which is why XAI's speed building data centers matters for Nvidia. ASIC economics get harder once customers realize the full stack matters. Baker says making the accelerator is only one piece. Customers then need CPUs, NICs, scale-up and scale-out switches, optics, protocols, and software.
>
> **Application**
>
> Baker applies this lens by comparing TPU token economics with Blackwell system transitions and by examining which customers can operationalize new hardware quickly. He expects short-term edge to come from full-stack coherence and deployment speed rather than silicon specs alone. On the demand side, he links declining token costs to deeper reasoning and broader automation in customer support, sales, and eventually robotics.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Google's TPU edge is real but may be temporary.
>
> **Q** Why does the Nvidia versus Google fight matter?
>
> **A** If Google is the low-cost token producer, it can suck economic oxygen from the ecosystem by pricing aggressively. Baker thinks Blackwell training and then inference could remove that advantage and make negative 30% margins painful for Google. Baker says serious AI users should test the $200/month highest tiers, not free tiers, and that the four leading labs are OpenAI, Gemini, Anthropic, and XAI.

> **2.** Blackwell is not just a chip; it is a system transition.
>
> **Q** Why was the Hopper-to-Blackwell move so hard?
>
> **A** The rack goes from air-cooled to liquid-cooled and from roughly 1,000 pounds to a much more complex deployment. Baker says it takes 6-9 months after deployment to work out bugs, which is why XAI's speed building data centers matters for Nvidia. He argues reasoning bridged an 18-month hardware gap: models went from 0-8% on ARC-AGI over four years to 8-95% in three months after OpenAI's first reasoning model, while pre-training waited for post-Hopper chips.

> **3.** ASIC economics get harder once customers realize the full stack matters.
>
> **Q** Can every hyperscaler replace Nvidia with its own chip?
>
> **A** Baker says making the accelerator is only one piece. Customers then need CPUs, NICs, scale-up and scale-out switches, optics, protocols, and software. He expects TPU and Trainium to matter, but fewer other ASIC efforts to succeed. He estimates 2027 TPU revenue around $30B, Broadcom gross margins at 50-55%, and Google effectively paying Broadcom about $15B; if TPU revenue were $50B in 2028, the payment could be ≈$25B.
