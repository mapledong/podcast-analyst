## Background

## Key Facts

> **F1** Slack direct listing June 20, 2019: reference price $26/share, first-day close $38.62 (≈$19.5B market cap) — up nearly 50% without traditional IPO primary issuance; Barry McCarthy engineered the Spotify-style DPO.

> **F2** Fiscal year ending January 2019: ≈$400M revenue (82% YoY growth); prior years ≈$220M and ≈$100M — Ben describes a triple-triple-double pattern from Stewart Butterfield's third act after Flickr and failed Glitch MMO.

> **F3** $800 million cash on balance sheet at filing; company unprofitable by choice due to ≈$8,000 CAC for paying customers with ≈13x LTV/CAC cited — freemium hosting costs depress margins versus profitable Zoom cousin.

> **F4** Teams sending 2,000 messages hit 93% retention threshold — Slack architected onboarding to reach this quickly, often under 1 day for larger teams; Zynga-style PMs optimized input metrics over output retention.

> **F5** Yahoo acquired Flickr for $22–25 million in 2004; independent life under a year before deal closed end of 2004. Stewart's three-year earn-out trauma bound co-founders for Slack retry — 'when you go through trauma together, you get bound together.'

## Mental Model · *Bottom-Up Unbundling of Microsoft*

> **Components**
>
> Stewart framed Slack as 'the 15-year slow-motion unbundling of Microsoft as the product of the suite for the enterprise.' Freemium lets teams adopt without CIO approval; viral spread inside orgs (Adobe had 9 paying teams pre-launch in February 2014). Gaming DNA from Glitch and IRC imported funnel analytics — input metrics (2,000 messages) not just output retention. DPO suits brands with $800M cash, existing mutual-fund holders, and no need for primary capital — opposite of cash-burning Uber IPO raising ≈$8B.
>
> **Application**
>
> Enterprise PLG requires a magic activation moment and freemium economics that subsidize host costs until conversion. When evaluating productivity tools, test bundling risk (Teams/O365) versus best-of-breed depth. Ben notes 'Zoom was actually the Slack we thought we had' on profitability — rebundling may come but Slack/Zoom won the 2010s unbundling wave. A+ grade requires expanding beyond messaging TAM.

## What Makes It Work

> Slack's moat is workflow embedding + archive gravity ('never leave Slack at Wave') and world-class PLG metrics (≈90% paid-customer year-one retention, ≈80% over five years cited). Flickr alumni engineering (Cal Henderson) and Stewart's influence brokering (Eric vote flip on Flickr pivot repeats at Slack). Search/archiving and integrations beat HipChat's technical-team niche. Weaknesses: Microsoft Teams free with O365; freemium hosting costs depress margins; 46x revenue multiple at episode time; Discord/Facebook Workplace fringe competition. Zoom comparison stings — 'Zoom was the Slack we thought we had' on profitability.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Slack is a consumer-growth playbook in enterprise.
>
> **Q** Why do gaming metrics matter?
>
> **A** Zynga-style PMs optimized input metrics over output retention. Slack found 2,000 team messages equals 93% non-churn; product nudged teams there fast — often under 1 day for larger orgs like RDO's 120-person company. Cisco and Microsoft never thought in funnel math — CIO dinners versus gaming-derived activation thresholds. HipChat had 1,000 companies within 4 months of 2010 launch but targeted dev teams only.

> **2.** DPO fits Slack's cap table, not Uber's.
>
> **Q** Why direct list vs IPO?
>
> **A** $800M cash, global brand, existing Fidelity/Tiger holders wanted to sell not buy new shares — DPO avoids dilution and lock-up. Uber needed ≈$8B primary; Slack didn't need primary capital. Barry McCarthy engineered Spotify-style direct listing: reference $26, close $38.62 (≈$19.5B cap), up nearly 50% day one without issuing new shares.

> **3.** Flickr sale was rational in 2004, unthinkable now.
>
> **Q** Should Stewart have sold Flickr?
>
> **A** No VC pressure to go big in 2004; $22–25M was dream outcome when Instagram later sold for $1B would be unthinkable. Yahoo earn-out trapped team three years while Web 2.0 passed — Stewart stayed extra months after Caterina left in 2008. Trauma bound co-founders: 'when you go through trauma together, you just want to keep working' — Slack's humane Glitch layoffs meant engineers would work for Stewart again.

> **4.** Teams is bundling, not product parity.
>
> **Q** Can Microsoft kill Slack?
>
> **A** Teams leverages O365 distribution; Slack's NYT ad welcomed competition but bundling rebundling risk is real. Ben Thompson coverage: good-enough free beats best-in-class paid for cost-conscious IT. Slack priced ≈$7–8/month per seat while Teams rides suite economics — structural disadvantage despite superior PLG metrics (≈90% year-one paid retention, ≈80% over five years).

> **5.** A+ requires expanding the TAM.
>
> **Q** Five-year grade criteria?
>
> **A** Ben/David: continue ≈82% growth plus broaden beyond group messaging (Notion/Dropbox path) or acquire as public company at ≈$19.5B cap. F scenario is world on Teams/Discord — $20B messaging ceiling without expansion. Slack at 46x revenue multiple at episode time needs TAM expansion; Zoom comparison stings because 'Zoom was the Slack we thought we had' on profitability.
