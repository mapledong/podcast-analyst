## Background

> Andrew Milgram co-founded Marblegate in 2008-09 with Paul Arway after years in distressed investing. The firm focuses on U.S. middle-market companies, sourcing loans from banks and doing restructuring work in-house. The conversation centers on a K-shaped economy, a Rapid Ratings data set, taxi medallions, tariffs, and why distressed investing is more than buying cheap high-yield bonds.

## Mental Model · *Ticker Tape vs Middle Market*

> **Components**
>
> The K-shaped economy is visible in company financials, not just household sentiment. Middle-market companies lack pricing power, sell into larger public customers, rely more on bank finance, and have less covenant flexibility.
