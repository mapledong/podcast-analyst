## Background

> Graham Weaver describes Alpine's three north stars: become the top-performing private-equity fund by MOIC, become the best place to work for top talent, and use the platform as a force for good. His personal story runs from a difficult childhood and wrestling to buying businesses out of business school and learning by doing. The practical Alpine model is a deliberate search for winnable games: avoid heavily banked auctions, find small companies that need new leadership, build the management team and holdco, then compound through operating improvement and add-ons.

>
> **A** Do a hard, operational game few firms want: succession-heavy small companies where the owner is retiring and the buyer must bring the CEO, systems, and culture. Alpine's stated target is 5x on every fund; Weaver says the last three funds are marked at 5x and the fourth is well on its way, after originally thinking 3x would put them in the conversation.

>
> **Q** Why obsess over MOIC instead of IRR?

> **A** A 5x MOIC goal encourages holding and building great businesses instead of selling winners quickly to show a high IRR or raise the next fund.
