## Key Facts

> **F1** Ranking metric: absolute dollar value added to acquirer market cap minus purchase price — not ROI multiple alone. Android ≈1555X ROI but ranks #4 because David says you can't eat ROI multiple — $16 billion is $16 billion whether 240X or 5X ROIC.

> **F2** Instagram (2012 ≈$1B): ≈$20B annual ad revenue attributed at recording — roughly two-sevenths of Facebook revenue; bidding war with Twitter cited. Free creator content, zero content COGS, reused Facebook ad sales infrastructure.

> **F3** Android acquired 2005 for ≈$50M; hosts attribute ≈$13B annual revenue contribution (≈$9B Play + search allocation) and ≈$77B net market cap contribution estimate — company now named after the acquisition.

> **F4** DoubleClick (2008 ≈$3.1B): Google Network ads largely DoubleClick+AdMob stack per episode research — initially underestimated by hosts when first analyzed.

> **F5** PayPal-eBay ($1.5B, 2002): spinout market cap $47B — $45.6B absolute dollar return, 28% annualized. booking.com ($135M, 2005): ≈$50B absolute return, ≈50% annualized over 15 years. Marvel ($4.2B, 2009): ≈$16B absolute return, ≈10% of Disney revenue.

## Mental Model · *Absolute Dollar Alpha*

> **Components**
>
> Best acquisitions create new business lines with near-zero marginal distribution cost for the acquirer — Instagram reused Facebook ad sales force (≈$20B revenue from ≈$1B buy); DoubleClick owned ad-serving rails (≈$3.1B buy). ROI multiples flatter small bases (Android $50M→1555X but #4 on absolute list); David: $16 billion is $16 billion whether 240X or 5X ROIC. David tags 7 of top 10 as business lines versus 2 products (Android, Maps suite) and 1 people+tech (NeXT). Content deals (Marvel ≈$16B absolute return, ESPN 15% over 35 years) rank below zero-marginal-cost tech unless repeatable franchises. Methodology: absolute dollar market cap contribution minus purchase price; majority deals only; enough time elapsed; March 2020 COVID volatility caveat.
>
> **Application**
>
> Corporate dev should score deals on incremental profit dollars at scale, not multiple on revenue — ask whether target must remain standalone business line to count. Facebook's Instagram/WhatsApp/Oculus spree versus Google X build-first culture: David's playbook — make these bets when distribution converts. VCs use list to justify platform acquisitions over organic build in winner-take-all markets.

## What Makes It Work

> Winning acquirers share open-loop distribution converting engagement into revenue lines. Instagram (≈$1B, 2012): ≈$20B annual ads revenue at recording — roughly two-sevenths of Facebook revenue; free creator content, zero content COGS. DoubleClick (≈$3.1B, 2008): Google Network ads largely DoubleClick+AdMob stack. Android (≈$50M, 2005): ≈$77B net market cap contribution, 1555X ROI but ranks #4 on absolute dollars — David: you can't eat ROI multiple. NeXT (≈$429M, 1997): Apple 'would not exist' without Jobs and NeXTSTEP→macOS/iOS lineage. Marvel (≈$4.2B, 2009): ≈$16B absolute dollar return, ≈10% of Disney revenue; Pixar ($7.4B) only ≈$2.3B net incremental (≈2% annualized). PayPal-eBay ($1.5B→$47B spinout): $45.6B absolute return, 28% annualized. booking.com ($135M, 2005): ≈$50B absolute return, ≈50% annualized over 15 years. ESPN (≈$200M, 1984): $10B+ revenue contribution, 15% annualized over 35 years. Weaknesses: attribution squinting (NeXT, Android, P.A. Semi 5% of chip differentiation); time-lagged outcomes; WhatsApp honorable mention too soon on revenue.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Business lines beat feature buys at scale.
>
> **Q** Category breakdown?
>
> **A** David tags 7 of top 10 as business lines versus 2 products (Android, Maps suite) and 1 people+tech (NeXT). Standalone P&L potential drives absolute dollar outcomes — Instagram ≈$20B ads through Facebook portal; DoubleClick ad-serving rails; Android ≈$77B market cap contribution from ≈$50M buy. Feature buys and talent acqui-hires rank lower unless they spawn entire revenue engines.

> **2.** Instagram is the revenue miracle.
>
> **Q** Why #3?
>
> **A** ≈$20B ads through Facebook portal at recording — roughly two-sevenths of Facebook revenue per Ben. Free creator content, zero content COGS, minimal incremental sales cost. Hosts prefer Instagram economics over YouTube bandwidth costs. Bidding war with Twitter cited in episode — Facebook's willingness to make big bets versus Google X build culture.

> **3.** Android wins ROI not rank.
>
> **Q** Why #4 not #1?
>
> **A** $50M → ≈$77B absolute market cap contribution estimate but ranks #4 on list because absolute dollars beat ROI multiples — David: you can't eat ROI multiple. ≈$13B annual revenue contribution (≈$9B Play + search allocation). Company renamed after tiny 2005 acquisition — mobile search default and Play billing toll booth secured.

> **4.** Make bets; don't only build.
>
> **Q** Facebook vs Google?
>
> **A** Facebook Instagram/WhatsApp/Oculus acquisition spree vs Google X build — hosts argue acquire when distribution converts; Google succeeded with YouTube/Android/DoubleClick buys too.

> **5.** Content is lumpy.
>
> **Q** Why Pixar honorable mention?
>
> **A** Pixar requires hit every year; Marvel/ESPN more predictable revenue — next-hit risk keeps Pixar out of top 10 despite cultural value.
