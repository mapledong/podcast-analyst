Jim Simons (codebreaker, Stony Brook builder) and mathematicians (Berlekamp, Ax, Mercer) found faint signals — right 50.75% of the time at huge frequency — not macro narratives. Lesson: edges are small, secret, and size-constrained — the opposite of Berkshire's public compounder model.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Edge is 50.75%, not prophecy.
>
> **Q** How can tiny accuracy beat Buffett?
>
> **A** Mercer: correct slightly more than half the time at huge frequency with leverage and risk controls compounds to ≈39% net for decades. 1990 first full winning year: +77.8% gross, +55% net. Fundamental investors need deep stories; RenTech needs statistical significance on millions of trades. Five-minute horizons stay predictable; three-year horizons revert to noise. 1990: +77.8% gross, +55% net first full winning year; 1991: +54.3% gross, +39.4% net. Ax burnout led Berlekamp to buy out Ax at ≈$27M AUM inflection.

> **2.** Capacity kills quant alpha.
>
> **Q** Why close Medallion at ≈$10B?
>
> **A** Slippage: models want size markets cannot fill — especially pre-equities in currencies/commodities. At ≈$5B AUM in 2003, external LPs expelled; carry already 36–44%. AUM grew purely from gains ($276M in 1994 → $3.8B by 2000) not new investors. Buffett's 'too big' problem shares logic but opposite solution. 2000: fund grew $1.9B to $3.8B purely from gains while tech bubble burst. 1994 +93% gross on $276M AUM scaling purely via reinvested gains.

> **3.** Medallion almost died multiple times.
>
> **Q** Was success inevitable?
>
> **A** Lenny Baum's bond losses (≈40%) expelled partner; early Medallion stumbled until Berlekamp bought Ax out at ≈$27M AUM. 25 years from IDA papers to working fund. RenTech started 50% VC / 50% currency trading — Axcom quant success hid inside VC-era firm. Berlekamp bought out Ax at ≈$27M AUM after early Medallion stumble. External LPs could not be incentivized out until carry hit 44%.

> **4.** Institutional products are deliberately weaker.
>
> **Q** Can I buy Medallion exposure?
>
> **A** No — outside LPs expelled 2003. RIEF returned ≈8.6% through Nov 2023, index-like; down 10.4% first 4 months of 2020 vs broader market. Documents disclaim Medallion returns; longer hold periods, lower Sharpe by design. RenTech monetizes reputation while guarding crown jewel. RIEF down 10.4% first 4 months of 2020 — mirror of broader market, unlike Medallion. Jim Simons philanthropy accelerated post-retirement 2009.

> **5.** Culture is campus, not Wall Street.
>
> **Q** Why Long Island PhDs?
>
> **A** Jim built Stony Brook with Rockefeller's $100M math push; recruited Berlekamp, Ax, Mercer, Brown. 5% management fee backed into ≈$800K infrastructure need. Secrecy, code ownership, extreme carry on closed capital — no AUM marketing treadmill. ≈$150–200B cash distributed to principals over 35 years. Initial capital under $4M; 25 years from IDA papers to Medallion working. Episode frames RenTech opposite Berkshire public compounder transparency model.
