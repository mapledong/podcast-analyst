## Background

> Matt Reustle breaks down Mercado Libre with Daniel Wu of Bristlemoon Capital, whose deep dive helped frame the episode. Wu traces MELI from Marcos Galperin's 1999 Buenos Aires garage — an eBay-inspired auction site — to a ≈$120 billion LatAm platform triple eBay's market cap at recording.
>
> The company operates commerce and fintech across 18 countries, with Brazil supplying roughly 55% of revenue and Mexico passing Argentina as the second-largest market in 2024. Wu walks the marketplace, Mercado Envios logistics, Mercado Ads, and Mercado Pago plus Mercado Credito fintech. Revenue grew 9x since end-2019 (≈55% CAGR) as commerce and fintech reinforced each other when one segment slowed.

## Key Facts

> **F1** At episode recording (September 2025), Wu cites a ≈$120 billion market cap — roughly 3x eBay — and Euromonitor data showing ≈26% LatAm e-commerce share in 2023, with Morgan Stanley arguing share exceeds the next 11 players combined. Galperin's early critics said LatAm culture would never buy unseen goods online.

> **F2** Mercado Libre's marketplace logged 100 million-plus unique buyers and $51.5 billion of GMV in 2024; Brazil contributes ≈55% of company revenue. Mercado Envios scaled fulfillment penetration above 50% while keeping net shipping economics near 3% of GMV despite heavy logistics investment.

> **F3** Revenue grew 9x from end-2019 through recording (≈55% CAGR) as Mercado Pago and Mercado Credito expanded fintech across 8 countries alongside commerce in 18 markets. The 2007 IPO at $18 per share compounded to 120x-plus by 2025 despite six 40%+ drawdowns; Mexico passed Argentina as the second-largest market in 2024.

## Mental Model · *Commerce–Fintech Flywheel*

> **Components**
>
> Use marketplace GMV to acquire buyers and sellers, attach Mercado Pago payments, then layer logistics, ads, wallet, and credit — each product feeds data and frequency back into the core marketplace. MELI built Mercado Pago escrow in 2003 and Mercado Envios from 2009 to solve low trust, card penetration, and patchy carriers that pure marketplace clones ignored. Wu describes MELI as Amazon retail plus Alipay, not a single-product marketplace.
>
> **Application**
>
> When commerce growth slows in one country, fintech can accelerate and vice versa; behavioral payment data improves Mercado Credito underwriting while fulfillment and Meli+ raise switching costs against Shopee and Amazon. Wu's core lesson is reinvestment: Meli keeps plowing margins into logistics and fintech density rather than optimizing for near-term earnings. Argentina's 40%+ contribution margins show what Brazil and Mexico could aspire to if share holds.

## Key Insights

> **1.** MELI survived by solving trust, payments, and shipping—not just listing items.
>
> **Q** Why did LatAm e-commerce not fail?
>
> **A** Wu recounts Galperin's early skepticism that LatAm would never buy unseen goods. MELI built Mercado Pago escrow in 2003, feedback systems, and Mercado Envios logistics from 2009—addressing low card penetration and patchy carriers that pure marketplace clones ignored. The dot-com crash nearly killed the company; surviving that vote taught management to prioritize sustainable unit economics over vanity user counts.

> **2.** Fintech is a second engine, not a side project.
>
> **Q** How big is Mercado Pago?
>
> **A** Wu describes MELI as Amazon retail plus Alipay: off-platform merchant acquiring, digital wallets, and Mercado Credito consumer and merchant lending. Fintech runs in 8 countries and kept growth strong when commerce faced macro pressure, with credit priced off marketplace behavior data. Mercado Credito's portfolio has scaled into the billions while Wu emphasizes short-duration loans and tightening underwriting in weaker credit tiers.

> **3.** Dominance varies by country but the platform compounds.
>
> **Q** Where is competition toughest?
>
> **A** Wu notes Argentina margins reflect long dominance, while Brazil and Mexico face Shopee and Amazon. Brazil fulfillment penetration topped 50% and net shipping economics stayed near 3% of GMV despite heavy investment—evidence the integrated stack can absorb competitive pressure. Wu argues Meli+ free-shipping thresholds and ads monetization add high-margin layers beyond take-rate competition on GMV alone.

## Chronology

>
