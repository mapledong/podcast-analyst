## Background

> Matt Reustle and Sean Barrett examine EQT's evolution from Wallenberg-backed Nordic roots into a global alternatives platform with private equity, infrastructure, real estate, and Asia capabilities. The conversation emphasizes EQT's operating model: local teams, governance-heavy value creation, and digital tools that accelerate sourcing and bolt-on execution. It also highlights the firm's ability to innovate in exit pathways and fundraising channels as private markets mature.

---

## Key Facts

> **F1** EQT manages roughly €270 billion in total AUM and around €140 billion in fee-paying AUM, with private equity still a large base and infrastructure/real estate now major contributors. The platform has grown materially since listing and strategic acquisitions.

> **F2** Historically, EQT reports strong fund outcomes, with gross MOIC and net IRR metrics near top-quartile levels across core strategies over long periods. This performance underpins fundraising momentum and LP retention.

> **F3** The firm raised about €75 billion in the prior cycle and targets roughly €100 billion in the next, supported by flagship step-ups, sovereign channel depth, and expanding private-wealth vehicles.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Local sourcing improves global scale.
>
> **Q** Why does EQT emphasize local offices?
>
> **A** Regional teams can access proprietary context and relationships, improving both origination quality and execution speed versus centralized-only models.

> **2.** Governance is a value-creation tool.
>
> **Q** How does EQT operationalize active ownership?
>
> **A** Board and management frameworks, plus digital support systems, create repeatable post-deal operating improvements rather than purely financial engineering.

> **3.** Exit innovation matters in slower markets.
>
> **Q** How does EQT maintain realization flexibility?
>
> **A** Beyond traditional IPOs and strategic sales, EQT has expanded secondary and continuation pathways to manage holding periods and realize value more selectively.
