## Key Insights

> **1.** FTX is Enron with a podcast and effective altruism brand.
>
> **Q** Why compare SBF to Skilling/Fastow?
>
> **A** Related-party lending (Alameda/FTX), mark-to-model token assets, auditor concentration, and charismatic narrative masking liquidity mismatch mirror Enron's SPEs and mark-to-market. Holiday special recorded with fresh outrage — Acquired uses historical episode as analytical framework for breaking news. SBF's FTX Ventures investments and Effective Altruism branding parallel Skilling's TED-talk intellectual veneer over mark-to-model books.

> **2.** Taylor Swift proves IP ownership beats platform reach.
>
> **Q** Why rank Swift among tech stories?
>
> **A** Re-recording masters to devalue Scooter Braun purchase is supply-side IP strategy — ≈$100M catalog economics and tour revenue control. Ben and David frame as media business case: owning masters yields pricing power streaming platforms cannot take away. Swift's Eras Tour 2023 gross exceeded $1B — vindicating re-recording strategy discussed before tour announcement finalized.

> **3.** NVIDIA's 2022 looked expensive until 2023 proved otherwise.
>
> **Q** What did hosts see pre-AI mania?
>
> **A** CUDA developer lock-in, data center GPU share, and gaming/crypto cyclicality discussed before ChatGPT mainstream moment. Holiday ranking treated NVIDIA as infrastructure winner — vindicated when data center revenue accelerated 2023–24. NVIDIA data center revenue exceeded gaming revenue by 2023 — holiday ranking proved prescient within two quarters. Jensen Huang's CUDA installed base cited as 10-year moat before generative AI inflection.

> **4.** Amazon vs Walmart is fulfillment geometry, not e-commerce share alone.
>
> **Q** Who won retail 2022?
>
> **A** Amazon's logistics capex and same-day ambition vs Walmart's store-as-warehouse (≈4,700 U.S. locations) — different network shapes for last mile. Neither "won"; episode compares capital intensity and margin structures omni players must sustain. Walmart marketplace GMV growth vs Amazon's 2022 layoffs in retail org showed divergent omni strategies.

> **5.** Acquired consciously chooses library over news.
>
> **Q** What meta lesson for listeners?
>
> **A** Hosts explain longer runtimes and fewer releases as investment in canonical episodes (Enron, Nintendo prep) that compound for years vs decaying news commentary. Holiday special is the exception where news gets airtime — filtered through historical frameworks. Patreon and ACQ2 interview feed extend brand without diluting main-season research bar. ACQ2 and meetups extend community LTV without shortening main episode runtimes below 120 minutes. Ben cites 2022 download growth >50% YoY for flagship episodes as validation of long-format bet.
