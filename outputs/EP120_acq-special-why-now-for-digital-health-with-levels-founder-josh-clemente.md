**Topics** Continuous Glucose Monitoring · Direct-to-Consumer Health · Metabolic Health
> ### Conclusion
>
> Josh Clemente — SpaceX life-support engineer turned Levels CEO — explains why continuous glucose monitors (CGMs) moved from diabetic therapeutics to proactive wellness: Abbott Libre and Dexcom hardware existed, but no consumer insights layer connected food, sleep, and stress to real-time glycemic response. Levels wraps Libre with software, telehealth prescriptions, and a $395/month beta program serving thousands with tens of thousands waitlisted — building the largest non-diabetic CGM dataset in roughly four years. The "why now" parallels Hims/Roman/Curology: reinterpret existing FDA pathways via telehealth rather than waiting for new regulation — disruptive versus sustaining paths in a $3.4 trillion US healthcare market still organized around payer billing codes.

## Background

> Ben and David wear CGMs throughout the interview, introduced by LPs Michael Mizrahi and Ben Grynol. Josh recounts SpaceX Hyperloop and Dragon life-support work (including emailing Elon for budget approval), keto experiments with colleagues that revealed opaque metabolic feedback, and founding Levels in 2017 when Libre was available but locked behind therapeutic prescribing. Discussion covers broken insulin feedback loops in metabolic disorder, oatmeal spiking Ben's glucose, David's Texas blackout stress spikes, Levels' wrap-around software model (not hardware), payer versus DTC business models, Apple Watch non-invasive CGM rumors, and Tesla analogies (Model S pricing down to Model 3). Alertive and Omada/Livongo illustrate the sustaining-innovation path inside insurance channels versus Levels' disruptive DTC wedge.

## Key Facts

> **F1** Levels charges $395 for its one-month beta program using Abbott FreeStyle Libre CGMs plus app analytics; thousands completed the program with an estimated 20,000–30,000-person waitlist at recording.

> **F2** Continuous glucose monitors existed therapeutically for 10+ years before Levels; Josh claims Levels assembled the largest dataset of non-diabetic CGM users in ≈4 years — faster than incumbents prioritized non-diabetic populations.

> **F3** US healthcare spending exceeds $3.4 trillion annually (≈$10,000+ per person); ≈$1 trillion flows through payers (insurers/employers/Medicare) with multi-year billing-code cycles versus DTC paths that launch in months.

> **F4** SpaceX Dragon cargo and crew milestones validated Josh's life-support engineering before Levels; he describes decision culture rejecting Boeing-style conservatism in favor of calculated hardware iteration.

> **F5** Telehealth prescribing (Hims, Roman, Curology parallel) reinterpreted existing regulations in 2015–2017 — no new CGM rule — enabling doctors to approve Libre via survey/text workflows for non-diabetic wellness seekers at scale.

## Mental Model · *Insights Layer on Existing Sensors*

> **Components**
>
> Levels separates hardware (Abbott/Dexcom CGMs), prescription gate (telehealth), and consumer UX (food logging, scores, education). Value accrues to software and data — like Apple Watch rings built atop sensor pack, not the sensor itself. DTC health "why now" = Stripe-for-prescription friction reduction plus marketing to wellness buyers outside payer funnels. Disruptive path (Levels) bypasses billing codes; sustaining path (Omada/Livongo via Alertive) improves experience inside insurance channels. Subscription retention and longitudinal data create moat if Apple ships non-invasive CGM — Levels bets on being interpretation layer, not chip maker.
>
> **Application**
>
> In regulated hardware markets, ask who owns the insight layer when commoditized sensors proliferate. Telehealth unlocks non-zero-sum expansion — wellness users need not deprive diabetics of devices. Evaluate pricing power at $395/month against education value; path downmarket requires capital from premium tier (Tesla Model S → Model 3). Watch Abbott/Dexcom forward integration risk.

## What Makes It Work

> Levels' edge in 2021 is timing and data — not manufacturing. First-mover dataset: Non-diabetic CGM correlations (food, sleep, stress) at scale incumbents ignored because payers wouldn't reimburse wellness — 7,000+ users in ≈4 years versus decades of diabetic-only clinical focus. Telehealth wrapper: Prescription compliance without clinic visits — regulatory interpretation shared with Hims/Roman wave that unlocked dermatology and ED before metabolic health. Community distribution: Acquired LP density, podcast marketing — DTC channels unavailable to device OEMs focused on clinicians and hospital formulary committees. Subscription and data lock-in: Longitudinal logs increase switching costs if insights improve over months (Meituan super-app analogy per David). Weaknesses: Hardware dependence on Abbott/Dexcom pricing and API access; Apple Watch CGM rumor could commoditize sensing; doctors may dismiss consumer data; $395 limits TAM until price drops; payer path uncertain for employer reimbursement.

## Key Insights

> **1.** CGMs broke the feedback loop — wellness is non-zero-sum with diabetes care.
>
> **Q** Why not leave CGMs to diabetics only?
>
> **A** Therapeutic use locked devices behind endocrinologists while metabolic dysfunction sat on a spectrum — millions optimizing blind. Josh argues giving healthy people CGMs doesn't deprive diabetics; it expands manufacturing scale and awareness. Ben's oatmeal spike and David's blackout stress data show actionable insights unavailable from annual labs.

> **2.** Why now is regulatory interpretation, not regulation change.
>
> **Q** What changed legally for Levels?
>
> **A** No new CGM rule — telehealth platforms (Curology, Hims, Roman) applied existing prescribing frameworks via async doctor surveys. HIPAA-compliant workflows let consumers "checkout" for prescriptions. Parallel wave unlocked dermatology, ED, and now metabolic monitoring without waiting for CMS billing codes.

> **3.** Levels captures software margin; Abbott captures sensor margin.
>
> **Q** Why won't Abbott build the consumer platform?
>
> **A** Device OEMs optimize FDA therapeutic trials and payer reimbursement — not Instagram ads and food-log UX. Josh estimates majority of consumer dollar flows to hardware suppliers today; Levels aims to invert over time via subscription and scale — but risks forward integration. Tesla analogy: premium Model S funds Model 3; don't assume GM ships the software stack.

> **4.** SpaceX decision culture enabled Levels' speed.
>
> **Q** What transferred from Hyperloop/Dragon to health tech?
>
> **A** Josh describes emailing Elon for budget, rejecting over-conservative aerospace norms, and iterating hardware under cost constraints — same first-principles spreadsheet mentality applied to metabolic health when Fitbit-for-glucose didn't exist. Complex regulated go-to-market in ≈4 years with 7,000+ users reflects that operating intensity.

> **5.** Apple non-invasive CGM would expand TAM for Levels, not kill it.
>
> **Q** What if Apple Watch adds glucose sensing?
>
> **A** Josh (no inside Apple knowledge) argues Apple won't build dedicated metabolic coaching — as iPhone cameras enabled Instagram, sensor commoditization helps interpretation layers. Historical pattern: each Apple sensor spawn ecosystem companies; non-invasive CGM would be massive unlock if accuracy sufficient.
