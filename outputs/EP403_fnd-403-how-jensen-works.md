## Key Insights

> **1.** Focus beats feature stuffing.
>
> **Q** What did NV1 teach Jensen?
>
> **A** Market wanted fastest graphics for games at fair price — not multifunction cards. 'Nobody goes to the store to buy a Swiss Army knife.' Positioning must answer Ogilvy's test: what the product does and who it is for.

> **2.** Bet the balance sheet on speed when cash is finite.
>
> **Q** How did Riva 128 save Nvidia?
>
> **A** With ≈6 months cash after the $1M Ecos purchase, Jensen built driver software before physical chips — shaving nearly a year. Cafeteria erupted when he revealed 30,000-unit STB Systems PO.

> **3.** Platform shifts require years of margin pain.
>
> **Q** Why did CUDA matter?
>
> **A** Four-year investment cut gross margin ≈45% to ≈35% while making GPUs programmable. Scientific, industrial, and AI customers compounded demand because every CUDA developer increased GPU value — a bet gaming-chip rivals missed.
