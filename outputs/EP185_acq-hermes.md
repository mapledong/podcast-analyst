## Key Facts

## Key Insights

> **1.** Waitlists are marketing balance sheet.
>
> **Q** Why not make more Birkins?
>
> **A** Unmet demand reinforces Veblen status — secondary market at $500K+ proves retail ≈$12K price 'low' for clearing market, but raising supply would shrink mystique. Jean-Louis chose waitlists over 1970s consultants' volume play. Prices rose ≈7% annually for 10 years (≈4–5% above inflation) — scarcity is intentional capital expenditure, not production failure. Queen Elizabeth wore silk carrés as head scarves in 1940s — decades of royal marketing. Thierry died 1878; son Charles-Emile apprenticed 20 years before leading.

> **2.** Family unity defeated Arnault.
>
> **Q** How did Hermès stay independent?
>
> **A** 1993 IPO sold 19% for liquidity among 80+ family members (≈$25M per heir in illiquid stock); family retained ≈81%. Arnault built stake from <$10B market cap, grew to 22.6% by 2011 — nearly entire 3.5% public float. H51 cooperative locked 50.2% for 20+ years (renewed to mid-2040s); French court fined LVMH but Arnault still profited ≈$5B distributing stake to family office. Arnault shrinking float to 3.5% public raised price via supply/demand mechanics. Emile expanded retail; sons-in-law Dumas Guérin Puech branches began.

> **3.** Craft scales through apprenticeship.
>
> **Q** How to 7,000 artisans without factories?
>
> **A** Savoir-faire transmitted artisan-to-artisan (≈2 years training); one person may finish entire Birkin — rejects split manufacturing LVMH brands use. Craftspersons sometimes receive items they made 10–20 years earlier for repair — full-circle quality feedback. Robert Dumas added artistic whimsy; Thierry added harness precision across six generations. Craftspersons receive own creations back for repair 10–20 years later — quality feedback loop. 1937 first silk carré; Queen Elizabeth popularized in 1940s.

> **4.** 1970s nearly killed the myth.
>
> **Q** What did consultants get wrong?
>
> **A** Mass fashion and democratization chased wrong customer until Jean-Louis returned from America honoring scarcity. Birkin born 1984 from Jane Birkin airplane sketch meeting. Silk was 55% of sales in 1988 vs leather 9% — category mix inverted to leather 43% today. Proves even 'unassailable' luxury rots without heir aligned to anti-scale ethos. Wonder Woman 84's $150 canvas tote cited as brand stewardship failure under wrong creative leadership. Jean-Louis retired 2006 at ≈$2B revenue; Axel Dumas CEO today.

> **5.** Hermès is anti-conglomerate thesis.
>
> **Q** Why compare to LVMH episode?
>
> **A** LVMH rolls up brands and centralizes marketing; Hermès single-brand craft at ≈$230B rejected efficiencies scale requires. Arnault identified Hermes below $10B — 8–9% of today's $230B — but could not absorb despite genius. 76% production in France, 85% sold abroad: selling sense of place to outsiders at premium. LVMH also bought ≈30–35% of Leica and 25% movement supplier for watch vertical integration. Family compares to Costco ketchup 3% test inverse — never discount luxury.
