## Key Facts

> **F1** Facebook acquired Oculus March 2014 for $2.3B total ($400M cash, $1.6B stock, $300M earnout).

> **F2** Kickstarter Aug 2012: $2.5M raised vs $250K goal; DK1 sold 4–5 units/minute at $300 on website post-campaign.

> **F3** Series A $16M (Jun 2013); Series B $75M led by a16z Dec 2013 — Marc Andreessen on boards of both Facebook and Oculus.

> **F4** ZeniMax lawsuit: $500M judgment Feb 2017 over Carmack/id IP; potential Rift sales injunction discussed.

> **F5** Valve/HTC Vive launched April 2016 with hand controllers and room-scale lighthouses — hosts say out-executed Rift.

## What Makes It Work

> Oculus under Facebook offered capital, brand for developers, and consumer Rift — but not durable hardware leadership. Carmack talent and DK2 quality impressed; consumer Rift March 2016 beat Vive by a week but lacked controllers until Dec 2016. Facebook's social VR demos compete with Valve's Steam distribution moat and HTC manufacturing. IP judgment and Palmer's political controversies (Nimble America) complicated brand. Integration moved team to Menlo — talent retention risk vs Valve's flat culture. Weaknesses: requires $1,000+ PC towers; mobile VR separate bet; horizontal Facebook may starve Oculus priority conflicts. Snap and Apple pursue camera/AR paths with clearer user bases. Kickstarter video featured Gabe Newell endorsing Oculus before Valve built Vive — ironic competitive arc. Palmer claimed Facebook accelerated hardware 5–10 years; consumer Rift still needed tower PCs at Madrona. ZeniMax board trivia (Cal Ripken, Moonves, Robert Trump) shows conglomerate weirdness. Mobileye hot take: Intel $15B bet on horizontal self-driving vs Tesla vertical — rhymes with Oculus/Facebook. Palmer Luckey left Facebook March 2017 per episode update — political controversy and integration strain.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Acquisition was FOMO on next platform.
>
> **Q** Why $2.3B pre-revenue?
>
> **A** Post-mobile scare (2012 pivot) — Facebook bought waves early (Instagram late, WhatsApp threat). VR years from consumer scale but cheaper than missing platform shift. Ben and David cross-reference prior Acquired episodes (Facebook IPO, Uber-Didi, Howard Schultz) and guest expertise where applicable. Grades and tech themes follow show format: acquisition category, what-would-have-happened-otherwise, and carve-outs close each episode on acquired.fm.

> **2.** Valve execution beat Facebook capital.
>
> **Q** Who won product 2015–2017?
>
> **A** Vive's hand tracking + room scale set bar; Oculus needed Facebook money to stay in game. Gabe Newell backed Oculus in Kickstarter video then partnered with HTC — brilliant end-around.

> **3.** Technology not business line for Ben.
>
> **Q** Acquisition category?
>
> **A** Defensive tech to ensure Facebook on all VR platforms — not revenue from Rift sales. Integrating deeper risks iMessage-style vertical bias.

> **4.** ZeniMax IP is costly footnote.
>
> **Q** Legal outcome?
>
> **A** $500M damages pushes effective price toward $2.8B; Carmack NDA/id code allegations. Board includes Cal Ripken, Leslie Moonves, Robert Trump per hosts' research digression.

> **5.** Social may be VR killer app.
>
> **Q** Is Zuckerberg right on social VR?
>
> **A** Rec Room and GoldenEye nostalgia support social games; Snap's camera AR reaches billions sooner. David: right thesis, poor execution to date — grade C with variance.
