## Key Insights

> **1.** Subscription paywalls rarely fit personality-led business media.
>
> **Q** Why not Substack paid tier when wife Puja pushed revenue?
>
> **A** Packy intrinsically knew ≈2–3% conversion at best unless crippling free posts; WSJ works via corporate subscriptions (finance audiences expensed). At ≈3,500 Twitter followers when Puja asked, he chose ads — Chris/MarketerHire sponsorship proved model. "Breakeven sustainable phase" lasted weeks before blowing past — common creator experience. Dirty secret: even 10% conversion requires heavy paywall handicap. Sponsorship deck still online — hosts link in show notes as artifact of early monetization design.

> **2.** Sponsored content works when it's the best marketing dollar — not adjacency.
>
> **Q** How is Not Boring journalism different from compromised integrity?
>
> **A** Packy discloses investments/sponsorships; audience is founders who want optimism + depth, not investigative takedowns (different viable audience for others). Ben: Thursday posts vary in personal interest but sponsor question is "obviously best way to get Packy-quality essay about my company." David: Packy's career path (IB → Breather ops → creator) can't be copied by jumping from investigative desk — integrity is fit-to-audience, not objectivity theater. Ben invested in Modern Treasury from sponsored post — content as diligence distribution. Sponsorship deck still online as artifact of early monetization design.

> **3.** Not Boring Capital unbundles the venture value chain.
>
> **Q** What does AngelList enable that a16z can't replicate cheaply?
>
> **A** Fund I ≈$9.99M / Fund II ≈$25M with ≈91 names — no associates, IR, or office; newsletter replaces sourcing and branding. David: "AWS for venture firm" not "Substack for VC." Specialization thesis: pie grows when labor splits (editor, fund admin, writer-investor). Portfolio construction embraces power law — fraud risk acknowledged, winners dominate. Saturday LP memo often becomes Monday public post.

> **4.** Process power prevents Morning Brew-style scaling.
>
> **Q** Could Packy hire writers and 10× output?
>
> **A** Hosts compare to Morning Brew (≈100 people, founders don't write daily letter but brand persists) — opposite path from Packy's solo corporation vision ("Power to the Person" — trillion-dollar one-person orgs within decades; Bitcoin cited as precedent). Process power: cannot hand off writing process — Economist pipeline failed to clone voice. Acquired admits same. Trade-off: ceiling on media output vs infinite margin per employee.

> **5.** Mega-VC acquisition is game-theoretic but Packy opts out.
>
> **Q** Why not join billion-dollar fund like a16z (Amazon/Kiva analogy)?
>
> **A** David: Amazon bought Kiva, shut 90% of clients — value to Amazon exceeded standalone. Packy going in-house would kill sponsor revenue (90% of business). Ben straw-man: management fees on billions exceed Not Boring income — Packy values control ("if it makes life worse, why do it?"). Andreessen path (super angel → institution) possible but Not Boring Capital scaling to billions open question — "can't slide in a little." Grade left to hosts; Packy optimistic throughout. Kindergarten Ventures parallel on transparent angel investing.
