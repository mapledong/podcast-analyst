## Background

> Matt Reustle revisits Toast with Sean Barrett of Counter Global, who holds the name as a top portfolio position. Barrett argues the 2020-era growth story has matured into a profitable platform: Toast serves roughly 160,000 U.S. locations with about 20% share, generates nearly $2 billion of recurring gross profit, and runs at roughly 35% EBITDA margins after years of burning capital to build hardware, software, and a field sales force. The conversation separates recurring subscription gross profit from reoccurring payments profit, walks through a revenue stack of ≈$300–500 per month in software plus a 49 basis-point net payments take, and explains why restaurants average seven active modules. Barrett highlights Toast Grow, an AI marketing product at ≈$500 per month that lifts restaurant revenue about 8% on average, and why high restaurant churn (≈15% annually) lets Toast win an outsized share of 100,000–120,000 new openings each year. He compares moats versus Square, Shopify, and DoorDash, cites a $10 billion gross-profit TAM, and discusses valuation near 18x 2027 GAAP earnings despite ≈25% gross-profit growth.

## Key Facts

> **F1** Toast serves roughly 160,000 U.S. restaurant locations with about 20% market share, generating nearly $2 billion of recurring gross profit at roughly 35% EBITDA margins after years of burning capital to build hardware, software, and a field sales force.

> **F2** Barrett estimates about $10,000 of annual gross profit per location from a 49 basis-point net payments take rate plus roughly $300–500 per month in software fees, with customers averaging seven active modules. Toast Grow, an AI marketing product at ≈$500 per month, lifts restaurant revenue about 8% on average — meaningful on ≈$1.3 million of average unit volume.

> **F3** Toast wins roughly half of new U.S. restaurant openings in an industry with ≈15% annual location churn (≈100,000–120,000 reopenings per year). Barrett cites valuation near 18x 2027 GAAP earnings against ≈25% gross-profit growth and a $10 billion gross-profit TAM; field research found zero owners willing to switch to free DoorDash POS.

## Mental Model · *Restaurant OS with Payments Attach*

> **Components**
>
> Lead with purpose-built hardware and multi-module software that runs daily operations; monetize through subscription gross profit plus reoccurring payments take as locations scale transaction volume. Barrett's field work in 30–40 San Francisco restaurants found zero owners willing to switch to DoorDash POS even if free, because Toast runs inventory, staff, and marketing — not just card capture. In 99%-retention categories challengers get only ≈1% annual churn to attack; restaurants churn ≈15% of locations yearly.
>
> **Application**
>
> High industry churn creates a land-grab on new openings, while module depth and operational integration raise switching costs versus Square terminals or free DoorDash POS offers. Toast Grow shows AI upsells can expand ARPU with measurable ROI — an average 8% revenue uplift that can double SaaS ARPU while giving owners a clear payback story. Barrett owns Toast as a core holding despite the stock pricing like a mature compounder.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Toast is an operating system, not a payments wrapper.
>
> **Q** How should investors classify Toast?
>
> **A** Barrett says Counter Global field work in 30–40 San Francisco restaurants found zero owners willing to switch to DoorDash POS even if free, because Toast runs inventory, staff, and marketing—not just card capture. Seven modules per customer and ≈$10,000 of gross profit per site support that framing.

> **2.** High churn markets favor challengers with superior product.
>
> **Q** Why can Toast keep gaining share?
>
> **A** In categories with 99% retention, challengers get only ≈1% annual churn to attack. Restaurants churn ≈15% of locations yearly, reopening 100,000–120,000 sites annually. Barrett says Toast wins about half of those new openings, compounding share without displacing every incumbent at once.

> **3.** AI upsells can expand ARPU with measurable ROI.
>
> **Q** What is Toast Grow?
>
> **A** Toast Grow is automated marketing priced around $500 per month. Barrett cites an average 8% revenue uplift for restaurants—meaningful on ≈$1.3 million of average unit volume—and says products like this can double SaaS ARPU while giving owners a clear payback story.
