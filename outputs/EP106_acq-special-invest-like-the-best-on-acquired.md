## Key Insights

> **1.** Investing and media are converging businesses.
>
> **Q** Why is ILTB the second iteration of OSAM's insight?
>
> **A** Patrick's father evangelized quant value research through media before podcasts existed. Today's managers face fee compression in long-only public equity while VC/PE absorbed billions with performance fees. ILTB amortizes research across a large audience — the Taylor Pearson/Morning Brew thesis that asset management is becoming media. The episode's 2020 recording date anchors these dynamics before subsequent CBA, streaming, and geopolitical shifts — but the structural incentives remain instructive for media and platform investors.

> **2.** Brand power equals quality times consistency times duration.
>
> **Q** Where does OSAM derive power?
>
> **A** High quality, low variance, over time — mirroring venture persistence at Sequoia/a16z. Performance alone commoditizes; brand lets OSAM launch Canvas, early-stage funds, and Infinite Powers without cold-starting trust each time.

> **3.** Canvas is a platform strategy decision, not a feature.
>
> **Q** License to competitors or keep proprietary?
>
> **A** Vertical integration preserves alpha but caps TAM; horizontal licensing scales research tooling like AWS but risks arming rivals. Patrick debated this explicitly — the answer shapes whether OSAM is a fund or a fintech platform.

> **4.** Narrative literacy is an investing edge.
>
> **Q** Why launch Infinite Powers podcast?
>
> **A** China tech discourse centers plot arcs ('where does this end?'); Western investing often lacks narrative framing. Infinite Powers applies story-structure analysis to business — extending ILTB's research flywheel into long-horizon strategic forecasting.

> **5.** SoftBank proved blunt capital distorts venture.
>
> **Q** What changed in private markets over 10 years?
>
> **A** Billions flooded venture with performance-fee psychology, raising prices and making $1B checks hard to deploy efficiently. Patrick contrasts OSAM's disciplined quant heritage with Vision Fund-style deployment that became an Acquired running joke for 2.5 years.
