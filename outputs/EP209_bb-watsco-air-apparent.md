## Background

> Watsco transformed from manufacturing into HVAC distribution in 1989. The company now has about $7.5B of revenue, roughly 700 sales branches, and 11-12% share of a $64B North American HVAC distribution market served by more than 2,000 distributors. About 90% of sales are in the U.S.; equipment is 70% of sales, parts and supplies 25%, and refrigeration 5%. Residential is 80% of end demand, and roughly 80% of residential demand is repair and replacement rather than new housing. Founder Albert Nahmad's acquisition model is partnership-oriented: more than 70 deals, often avoiding auctions, including the 2009 Carrier joint venture that nearly doubled revenue by adding about $1B.
>
>  The Carrier relationship is both strength and risk. It gave Watsco scale through the 2009 joint venture and later regional JVs, while Carrier still accounts for about 65% of purchases. That alignment lets Watsco consolidate distribution without alienating the manufacturer, but it also concentrates supplier exposure. The company's acquisition style — minority stakes, joint ventures, and avoiding auctions — reflects a trust-based roll-up rather than financial engineering alone.

## Mental Model · *Decentralized Distribution Compounder*

> **Components**
>
> Combine fragmented local branches, manufacturer partnerships, replacement demand, and disciplined acquisitions. Scale improves availability without destroying local entrepreneurship.
>
> **Application**
>
> WSO deserves quality treatment if it keeps taking share in Sunbelt markets, executes M&A, and benefits from regulatory mix upgrades. Supplier concentration with Carrier and housing cycles are key risks.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Replacement demand stabilizes revenue.
>
> **Q** Why is Watsco less housing-sensitive than it looks?
>
> **A** Residential is 80% of demand, but roughly 80% of that is repair and replacement rather than new construction.

> **2.** M&A is relationship-based.
>
> **Q** How has Watsco consolidated?
>
> **A** It has done more than 70 deals and often uses joint ventures or minority structures, including Carrier partnerships where Watsco owns 80% and Carrier 20%.

> **3.** Scale plus local works.
>
> **Q** What is the operating formula?
>
> **A** Large purchasing power and 700 branches support availability, while operating companies keep local customer relationships.
