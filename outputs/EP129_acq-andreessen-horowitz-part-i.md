> **Components**
>

Survive until market catches up — Opsware at $28M market cap with $63.5M cash + $20M EDS contract. Spyglass/University of Illinois $8M IE license. Jim Clark $4M of $15M net worth into Netscape. Pets.com Loudcloud customer illustrates bubble CapEx trap. Thunderbird email client footnote; Benchmark Andy Rachleff only friendly partner.
>
> **Application**
>

## Key Insights

> **1.** Mosaic growth proved mass-market internet.
>
> **Q** Why did academics resist Mosaic?
>
> **A** Internet elite wanted research-only purity — Marc: 'only smart people should use internet.' Mosaic went 12→1,000→10,000→1M users in ≈12 months on dial-up TAM. Al Gore's 1991 bill funded Marc's $6.25/hour salary. Episode ties to broader Acquired themes: concentrated ownership, DPI over IRR marketing, and founder quality sensed before metrics confirm. The episode ties numbers to primary sources (Chernow, Russo, Chang interviews, company filings where cited) rather than secondary hype.

> **2.** Free browser rewrote software economics.
>
> **Q** Why wasn't Netscape sold in boxes?
>
> **A** NCSA retained Mosaic IP; Netscape inverted model — free browser, paid servers (aggregation theory precursor). Microsoft licensed Spyglass/Mosaic for IE, paid only ≈$8M total — bundling killed Netscape distribution.

> **3.** Benchmark feud seeded a16z positioning.
>
> **Q** What happened at Loudcloud Series A?
>
> **A** Marc's $6M personal check raised post-money; Benchmark partner Byrne attacked Ben's CEO fitness in front of team. Marc/Ben alienated from Benchmark except Andy Rachleff — explicit later counter-positioning vs. classic VC.

> **4.** Opsware proved Ben can salvage CapEx disasters.
>
> **Q** How recover from Loudcloud?
>
> **A** Sold servers for $63.5M; rebuilt provisioning software for EDS ($20M/year contract). Stock at $0.35 (≈$28M cap) with $63.5M cash — five years later HP paid ≈$1.6B. Doug Leone called Ben to learn turnaround mechanics.

> **5.** Super-angel density preceded firm formation.
>
> **Q** Why start a16z in 2009?
>
> **A** While Sequoia wrote RIP Good Times, Marc/Ben did ≈36 angel deals — most active 'VCs' who weren't a firm. Zuckerberg quote: Marc only advisor saying don't sell to Yahoo. IM to Marc: 'We ought to start a VC firm.' The episode ties numbers to primary sources (Chernow, Russo, Chang interviews, company filings where cited) rather than secondary hype.
