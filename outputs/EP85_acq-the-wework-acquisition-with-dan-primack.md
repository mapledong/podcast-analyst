## Background

> Act I: Adam Neumann (Israeli Navy, dyslexic, Baruch MBA) fails collapsible heels and Krawlers baby clothes; meets 6'8" Miguel McKelvey at a 2008 Dumbo party. Greendesk subleases eco-branded space during financial crisis — landlord buys it for ≈$3M (2010). WeWork relaunches in SoHo: Joel Schreiber buys ~⅓ at ≈$45M valuation for ≈$15M cash, funds SoHo buildout with ≈100% occupancy per quarter-floor. Act II: Benchmark leads ≈$17M Series A (2012, ≈$97M post — higher than Uber's A). PepsiCo and corporates adopt early. Artie Minson (ex–Time Warner Cable CFO) hired 2015; cable buildout-losses-then-cash-flow analogy misapplied. SoftBank Vision Fund (2017): Masa sketches $4B ($1.3B primary, $1.7B secondary including Adam) at $20B; 20:1 super-voting shares. Act III: We Company trademark sold to Adam for ≈$5.9M; Lord & Taylor building ≈$850M; IPO S-1 exposes conflicts; Adam out Sept 24; IPO pulled; SoftBank acquires control with $185M consulting fee, ≈$3B tender, ≈$1.5B new equity.

## Key Facts

> **F1** SoftBank's 2017 WeWork investment: ≈$4B total — ≈$1.3B primary for international expansion, ≈$1.7B secondary to existing shareholders (including Adam) at ≈$20B valuation; deal sketched in ≈12 minutes on iPad.

> **F2** Benchmark led ≈$17M Series A in summer 2012 at ≈$97M post-money — higher post-money than Uber's Series A; Bruce Dunlevie compared Adam to 1997 eBay intangibility.

> **F3** WeWork S-1 (August 2019) ran hundreds of pages with nested LLC structures, 20:1 super-voting shares, and ≈$500M JP Morgan personal loan to Adam backed by WeWork stock.

> **F4** IWG (Regus parent) comparables cited: ≈$3.3B revenue, ≈$200M operating profit, ≈$4.6B market cap — Ben's "Bizarro World" steady-state versus WeWork's ≈$8B rescue valuation.

> **F5** By October 2019 SoftBank rescue: ≈$185M Adam consulting fee, up to ≈$3B tender (≈$1B for Adam), ≈$1.5B new equity at ≈$11/share (≈$8B net company value); ≈14,000 of 15,000 employees' options underwater at ≈$19.19 tender reference.

## Mental Model · *Vision Fund Capital Arms Bad Governance*

> **Components**
>
> SoftBank needed vehicles to deploy $100B in <5 years — WeWork could absorb billions unlike Slack. Masa's "be crazier" feedback reinforced Adam's community narrative over unit economics. Board faced Kobayashi Maru: reject billions or accept Adam-friendly terms. Secondary sales let Adam derisk while company needed primary capital for leases. Real estate economics (long liabilities, short revenue) dressed as SaaS multiple; Artie's cable analogy justified losses as buildout. Price discovery failed with one buyer (SoftBank) setting marks up and down.
>
> **Application**
>
> When single LP/strategic can deploy mega-checks, governance norms break — secondary-heavy rounds reward founders before durability proven. Compare mature profitable comps (IWG) when "tech" multiples applied to lease arbitrage. Rescue financing often PE playbook (debt + turnaround), not venture outcome.

---

## What Makes It Work

> WeWork's real early edge was design-forward community space making freelancers feel legitimate — not green desks or novel subleasing. Product-market fit for startups/freelancers post-2008 was genuine; PepsiCo corporate desks validated enterprise. Community moat eroded as scale, Adam conflicts, and burn overshadowed culture. SoftBank's rescue may salvage real estate network and brand, but competitive advantage versus IWG/Regus is unproven at steady state — episode suggests ~IWG economics if rationalized. Adam's building purchases leasing back to WeWork destroyed alignment. Rescue is PE turnaround, not venture win. IWG ≈$4.6B comp versus ≈$8B rescue. Adam trademark ≈$5.9M, Lord & Taylor ≈$850M, G650 ≈$60M as symbols of capital misallocation. Dan Primack Pro Rata sourced much reporting. Vision Fund II fundraising pressure drove 2019 $16B round collapse. JPMorgan $500M personal loan against shares. ≈15,000 employees, ≈14,000 underwater options at tender.

## Key Insights

> **1.** First valuation playbook was non-VC from day one.
>
> **Q** Why $45M seed from a real estate developer?
>
> **A** Adam pitched Brooklyn developer Joel Schreiber at a building auction — not Sand Hill Road. ≈$15M bought ~⅓ with lease as collateral. Ben: unfamiliar pitch to non-VC at non-tech valuation — pattern repeated with SoftBank later.

> **2.** SoftBank secondary changed incentives before product maturity.
>
> **Q** What broke after Masa's 2017 check?
>
> **A** ≈$1.7B secondary let insiders cash out while company still needed lease capital. Adam took ≈$500M JP Morgan loan against shares, bought buildings WeWork leased, trademark deal ≈$5.9M. Growth-for-growth replaced Greendesk-era product insight.

> **3.** S-1 was first price discovery — market said no.
>
> **Q** Why did IPO fail after $47B private marks?
>
> **A** Governance (20:1 votes, party school passages, org chart maze), losses without steady-state path, Adam conflicts. Attempted fixes Sept 13 insufficient; Adam out Sept 24; IPO pulled. Only SoftBank remained as buyer — classic single-buyer pricing.

> **4.** WeWork was tragedy of system, not solo villain.
>
> **Q** Is Adam solely to blame?
>
> **A** Episode opens as Greek tragedy — predetermined forces. Boards, SoftBank LPs, bankers (Jefferies, Goldman), and Benchmark each enabled stages. Dan Primack's reporting chronicled incentives; grade separates Adam decisions from SoftBank's Oct 2019 save.

> **5.** Rescue is PE turnaround, not venture win.
>
> **Q** Why grade SoftBank B−?
>
> **A** Saved from zero vs IWG ≈$4.6B comp, but ≈$6.5B+ SoftBank Corp/Vision Fund deployed for ≈$8B value — good money after bad. Unconsolidated structure may protect SoftBank telecom debt ratings; no CEO named. Vision Fund masquerading as VC unmasked.
