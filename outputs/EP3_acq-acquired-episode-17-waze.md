## Key Facts

> **F1** Google acquired Waze June 2013 for ≈$1.0–1.3B (mostly cash); team remained in Israel — Facebook deal failed partly over relocating HQ to Menlo Park versus preserving Israeli engineering hub.

> **F2** Waze Oct 2011 Series C: $30M at ≈$200M valuation from Kleiner Perkins and Horizons Ventures with 7M users; Dec 2010 had 2M users and 250M km logged on real city streets versus static competitor map data.

> **F3** Apple iOS 6 (2012) replaced Google Maps; Tim Cook public apology — unprecedented; Scott Forstall refused to sign letter and was later fired; Waze listed as alternative in apology.

> **F4** Apple rumored ≈$500M Waze talks post-Maps flop; Facebook ≈$1B acquisition reported for weeks before Google won — bidding drama accelerated after Maps failure.

> **F5** Turn-by-turn navigation apps cost $50–100 (Garmin/TomTom); Waze gave navigation free because probe data was the product — David compares to Instagram beating $3 Hipstamatic, LinkedIn free resumes, Skype free calls; Ben: a billion dollars is not a talent acquisition.

## Mental Model · *Free Product, Paid Data*

> **Components**
>
> When probe data has platform value, give consumer product away — Waze offered free turn-by-turn nav while Garmin/TomTom charged $50–100. Users report police, accidents, traffic; Google Maps ingests probe fleet. David compares pattern to Instagram beating $3 Hipstamatic, LinkedIn free resumes, Skype free calls, WhatsApp free texts — initial users often 'cheapos' avoiding paid alternatives. M&A resolves mobile war externalities: Apple needed Waze after Maps miss; Google could not let Facebook own social+driving graph.
>
> **Application**
>
> Evaluate mapping/logistics startups on probe density and refresh rate (250M km logged Dec 2010; 7M users Oct 2011), not consumer subscription revenue. Defensive M&A pricing should model counterfactual platform loss — iOS default maps/search — not standalone Waze DCF. Israel HQ retention was deal term, not detail.

## What Makes It Work

> Waze moat was live crowdsourced incident layer on community-maintained basemap — 250M km logged by Dec 2010 on real routes versus top-down Garmin/TomTom tiles. Social login and gamified reporting ('you drive with friends') created faster police/traffic updates than passive probes alone. Israeli engineering hub preserved post-deal — talent retention clause beat Facebook Menlo Park integration demand. Google integration fortified Maps real-time routing; denied Apple ($500M rumors) and Facebook (≈$1B press cycle) ownership of commute attention. Free nav destroyed $50–100 paid app market while harvesting probe fleet — data asset upends industry business model. Weakness at sale: modest revenue versus ≈$1B price; Apple Maps eventually recovered quality. Ben contrasts Waze strategic data buy with ≈$1B GM Cruise — Waze delivered Maps value quickly.
