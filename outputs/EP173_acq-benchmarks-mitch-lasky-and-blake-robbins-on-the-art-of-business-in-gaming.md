## Mental Model · *Business Model Channels Creativity*

> **Components**
>
> Creative industries require business-side partnership — LVMH parallel. Arcade quarter-drop (≈$7B peak) → packaged retail → digital platform (Steam, Valve ≈$8B private) → live service/forever games → cloud (thin vs thick client cycles). Platform publishers own payment rails and captive audiences — Peloton-like selection bias into subscriptions. Venture pattern Mitch describes: distribution advantage upfront → differential profits vs competitors → reinvest in content/forever-game loops — won't fund isolated League-of-Legends-scale studio without ecosystem power. Hits-driven fading: Miyamoto repeats proven story worlds (Mario/Zelda). Rights to publish from chat/social graph rare (QQ yes, Facebook 11 failures). Web3: ownership narrative conflicts with dev monetization unless dynamic economy (Roblox model). Riot early Craigslist/Dota forum growth hack cited as one-to-one community redirect superior to paid UA.
>
> **Application**
>
> Founders: map business model before creative pitch — console game startup is weird unless cross-play era changes math. Investors: evaluate reinvestment ROI on retained MAU vs sequel risk. Incumbents: leveraging customer relationship into publishing requires trust and payment infrastructure, not audience size alone (Acquired can't launch Acquired Games). Watch cloud gaming for next model shift — Nintendo Switch continuity bull case vs re-aggregation each generation.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Models precede creativity in gaming.
>
> **Q** Why launch Gamecraft?
>
> **A** Mitch says common narrative reverses causality — business models guide where creative water flows for 50 years, not genius then models. LVMH episode parallel: business and creators must collaborate in creative industries. Final Fantasy XIV live service exemplifies model-driven design.

> **2.** Distribution beats content democratization.
>
> **Q** Why isn't every creator a publisher?
>
> **A** 2023: distribution is king — Steam/consoles/Tencent have payment rails and committed users; Fortnite beats iPhone monetization. QQ leveraged chat into games; Facebook tried 11 times. Having podcast audience ≠ right to publish games; Discord was failed game company first.

> **3.** Forever games change reinvestment math.
>
> **Q** How do live titles differ from boxed games?
>
> **A** Successful forever games reinvest content spend into existing players vs shipping new SKUs — esports becomes leveraged marketing (100M+ viewers, lower entry barriers than golf). Mitch seeks distribution edge first, then reinvest differential profits.

> **4.** Riot's origin is a growth-lesson.
>
> **Q** What beat Dota's unmaintained PMF?
>
> **A** Clear PMF in mod unloved on forums — Riot shipped similar gameplay/new IP; Reddit threads one-to-one redirected community. Apple Podcasts parallel: heat without business prioritization leaves opening for Spotify-style entrants.

> **5.** Web3 skepticism from industry insiders.
>
> **Q** Is crypto gaming the future?
>
> **A** Roblox succeeds as 'Web3' without blockchain — compounding on trades, pay-to-win breaks social-status games. Speculation kills economic dynamism. Gamecraft ends hopeful but measured; capital attracted without usage/care in many corners.
