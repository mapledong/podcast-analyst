## Background

> John Zito describes the evolution from a credit market where a $1.4B fund was enormous in 2002-03 to Apollo as a just-under-$800B asset manager. The firm merged with Athene to combine third-party asset management with a large proprietary insurance balance sheet. The conversation is about private credit at investment-grade scale: annuity flows, origination, securitization, Europe versus the US, and how deals for companies like Intel, BP, Air France, and AB InBev change what an alternative manager can be.

## Mental Model · *Duration-Matched Origination Machine*

> **Components**
>
> Athene is the strategic center, not an add-on. It gave Apollo long-duration liabilities and more than $300B of proprietary capital. That changed the model from agent-only asset management to a principal-focused platform with a first-loss mindset and daily origination demand. Investment-grade private credit is becoming normal for blue-chip issuers. For a company with $100B of debt, a $5B Apollo financing can be a diversifier rather than a distress signal.
