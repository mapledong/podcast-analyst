## Key Facts

> **F1** Andreessen Horowitz Fund I: $300M (December 2009) — very large first-time fund; 2.5% management fee ≈ $7.5M/year; founders took no salary ≈2 years.

> **F2** September 2009: $50M into Skype spinout for 1.8% alongside Silver Lake — returned ≈$153M (≈3×) when Microsoft bought Skype ≈$8.5B (≈18 months).

> **F3** Okta Series A lead → ≈18–20% ownership; ≈$6B IPO valuation (≈$33B market cap later in episode context).

> **F4** Pre-firm angel track: 36 deals in 3 years, ≈$200K max check — a16z later formalized seed program without board seats to reduce signaling fear.

> **F5** Skype investment = 16.7% of $300M fund in one company — violated classic ownership-percentage norms but returned quick DPI. (2021 episode context).

## Mental Model · *Platform VC vs. Partnership VC*

> **Components**
>
> Benchmark: small partnership, equal carry, no platform — counter-positioned Kleiner. a16z counter-counter-positions: spend management fees on services so technical founders become 'professional CEOs' without replacement. Marketing as new VC discipline ('introduced marketing to VC'). Alphabetized 'Andreessen Horowitz' beats 'Benchmark' in press lists. Signaling risk overblown — Instagram raised Benchmark A after a16z chose PicPlz competitor. Apptio first check with Madrona co-invest. PicPlz vs Instagram signaling lesson. Charlie Rose interview announced firm before product was obvious. Paul Holland 60-70 investment critique aged poorly. WORK ticker references Slack a16z seed path.
>
> **Application**
>
> When evaluating VC brands, ask if fees fund LP-aligned DPI or founder services that increase ownership value. a16z bet that multi-stage + media + regulatory lobbying beats pure partnership lean model at scale.

## What Makes It Work

> a16z wins on founder selection of brand — Marc's Netscape mythos, Ben's Hard Things credibility, and visible services. Skype/Okta prove non-traditional checks (growth PE in Fund I, enterprise SaaS) can return fund-scale outcomes. Weaknesses: Instagram/PicPlz miss; Skype concentration; criticism on valuations and fund size arms race they started. Incumbents copied platform play. Versus Sequoia: a16z publicly counter-positioned Benchmark, rarely Sequoia ('come at the king, you best not miss'). Media strategy extended beyond launch: New Yorker 'Tomorrow's Advance Man' (2015), a16z podcast network, and regulatory lobbying as platform extensions. Fund evolution: crypto fund, bio fund, growth rounds — each counter-positioning prior generation's scope limits. Criticism that a16z overpaid at high valuations became industry norm as multi-billion-dollar funds proliferated. Kindergarten Ventures LP disclosure illustrates ongoing Sand Hill interconnection. Silver Lake Sand Hill neighbor facilitated Skype deal. Sequoia RIP Good Times memo contrast. Jeff Jordan PayPal/eBay operating credibility on a16z bench. Crypto fund later cycle repeats 'introduce marketing to VC' pattern. Fund size creep criticism acknowledged in episode meta-commentary.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Management fees should buy founder outcomes.
>
> **Q** Why hire 60+ staff at a VC?
>
> **A** Marc/Ben saw VCs telling founders to take $50K–60K salaries while partners earned millions in fees regardless of DPI. a16z redeployed ≈$7.5M/year into recruiters, PR, regulatory — synthetic network for technical CEOs. Episode ties to broader Acquired themes: concentrated ownership, DPI over IRR marketing, and founder quality sensed before metrics confirm. The episode ties numbers to primary sources (Chernow, Russo, Chang interviews, company filings where cited) rather than secondary hype.

> **2.** Signaling effect hurts VCs more than founders.
>
> **Q** Does seed without Series A follow-on kill companies?
>
> **A** Instagram seeded as PicPlz competitor; a16z passed Instagram — Benchmark led Series A, Facebook acquired ≈$1B. Signaling narrative overstates VC follow-on importance.

> **3.** Skype trade was DPI engineering.
>
> **Q** Why $50M for 1.8%?
>
> **A** Classic VC math says need 20% ownership — a16z bought ≈1.8% of Skype for $50M (16.7% of fund) and returned ≈$153M quickly. Proved firm could do growth PE, not only classic Series A.

> **4.** Brand is distribution.
>
> **Q** Why 'Andreessen Horowitz' not 'a16z' officially?
>
> **A** Alphabetical press ordering, Charlie Rose launch, New Yorker profile — Marc as 'Tomorrow's Advance Man.' a16z used in media; legal name retained on Sand Hill signage.

> **5.** Ovitz model for conflict-heavy industries.
>
> **Q** Where did services idea originate?
>
> **A** Michael Ovitz joined Loudcloud board after Disney — Hollywood agent packaging applied to startup CEO support. Punch competitors in mouth metaphor for competitive fundraising.
