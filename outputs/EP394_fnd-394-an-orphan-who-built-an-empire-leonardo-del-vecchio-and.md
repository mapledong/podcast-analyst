## Background

> Senra covers Leonardo Del Vecchio's Luxottica empire — eyewear's Citizen Kane. Father died before birth; mother placed him in an orphanage. At 14 he apprenticed as a metal engraver; at 25 he opened a workshop in Agordo, offered free land to workers, and lived next door starting shifts at 3 a.m.
>
> Over 50 years he acquired Ray-Ban (washed-up $19 gas-station aviators), Oakley, Persol, and LensCrafters via a $1.4 billion hostile bid for US Shoe — five times his company's size. Plants produce 400,000 pairs daily across 180–230 steps; 80% of LensCrafters displays carry Luxottica frames. He listed on NYSE when Italian IPOs were 'impossible,' then merged with Essilor.

---

## Key Facts

> **F1** Del Vecchio's Agordo factory scaled to 400,000 pairs per day across 27,000 models and 180–230 manufacturing steps — lasers, chemical baths, and massive automation with profits reinvested first into R&D, never skimped.

> **F2** 1995 hostile takeover of US Shoe for $1.4 billion — target five times larger — stripped conglomerate until only LensCrafters remained, then filled stores with Luxottica frames; ≈80% of LensCrafters displays later carried in-house brands.

> **F3** Ray-Ban deal paid $645 million for a brand doing ≈$2 billion annual sales under Luxottica; Armani licensing (1988) took 5% equity and turned medical devices into fashion — Prada, Gucci, Chanel licenses followed.

## Mental Model · *Own the Stack from Alloy to Shelf*

> **Components**
>
> Start wholesale parts, integrate design, factories, brands, and retail. Hostile courage when vertical gap appears. License fashion houses for equity stakes. Automation and 3 a.m. leadership set pace; competitors stay fragmented opticians.

> **Application**
>

## Key Insights

> **1.** Vertical integration is the entire strategy.
>
> **Q** What was Del Vecchio's simple equation?
>
> **A** Make stuff, sell stuff — why doesn't everyone buy it? He moved from parts to brands to LensCrafters' 950+ stores globally. Jeff Cole noted rivals spend years figuring how to kick Luxottica off sunglass hut and mall shelves.

> **2.** Hostile scale jumps beat incremental wholesale.
>
> **Q** Why buy US Shoe five times your size?
>
> **A** Wholesale dominance hit a ceiling; retail owned the customer. The $1.4 billion 1995 bid looked crazy but broke US Shoe until only LensCrafters remained — then he stuffed frames into every display.

> **3.** Fashion licenses transform category economics.
>
> **Q** How did Armani change eyewear?
>
> **A** 1988 Giorgio Armani deal with 5% Luxottica equity turned glasses from medical necessity to self-expression — like Beats headphones later. Ray-Ban revived from $19 gas-station aviators to ≈$2 billion annual brand sales.
