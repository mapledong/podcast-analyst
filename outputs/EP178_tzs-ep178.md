## Key Insights

> **1.** Craft is the moat; fashion is the trap.
>
> **Q** Why does Kevin stress manufacturing quality?
>
> **A** Collectible buyers punish defects permanently — unlike fashion, there is no seasonal reset. Pop Mart's premium multiples assume sculptor-led IP and tight QC. Scaling production without craft discipline copies margin but destroys community NPS.

> **2.** IP pipeline is years, not quarters.
>
> **Q** How does Pop Mart differ from pure fashion?
>
> **A** DIMOO and SKULLPANDA incubated through creator relationships and world-building — not trend-chasing SKUs. Kevin's culture-founder role bridges Bilibili fandom and factory floors. Investors should model IP maturation curves, not same-store fashion comps.

> **3.** Culture-founders align community and supply chain.
>
> **Q** What is Kevin's structural role?
>
> **A** He translates IP soul into manufacturable specs — serving both platform (Bilibili) and brand (Pop Mart). That linkage is hard to hire; losing it risks generic mascots. 9992.HK long-watch thesis includes artist roster depth, not just store count.
