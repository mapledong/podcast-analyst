## Key Insights

> **1.** Mastercoin rejection birthed Ethereum.
>
> **Q** Why not build on Bitcoin?
>
> **A** Vitalik proposed general-purpose scripting to Mastercoin — told to finish core functions first. November 2013 white paper emailed to crypto insiders; January 2014 Miami conference announcement. Colored Coins and Israel trip showed need for new chain. Parallel to Mark Zuckerberg 10 years earlier: young founder building what existing platform wouldn't. Vitalik emailed November 2013 white paper draft to small crypto insider group before Miami 2014 launch. Mastercoin told Vitalik to finish core functions before general-purpose scripting.

> **2.** Foundation structure was regulatory arbitrage.
>
> **Q** Why Swiss non-profit?
>
> **A** SEC former commissioner Grundfest accepted gas/commodity analogy — ether needed utility (pay validators) to avoid securities classification. C-corp founders left; Vitalik held moral authority at 20 during $18.3M crowd sale. Planned $5M sale became $18.3M in two weeks selling ≈60M ether. Crowd sale July 22–Aug 2014: $2.2M in first 12 hours; Vitalik hugged yoga ball on Swiss terrace at age 20. Anthony and Joe repaid from crowd sale proceeds funding early development team.

> **3.** ICOs were killer app for bootstrapping.
>
> **Q** Why 2017–18 bubble?
>
> **A** Every token sale needed ETH — demand loop like Silk Road for Bitcoin. 2016: 64 ICOs >$100M; H1 2018: 2300 ICOs ≈$11B. ETH peaked ≈$1,350 (≈$100B cap) at start of 2018. Utility for ETH even when many ICOs were scams. ETH peaked ≈$1,350 (≈$100B cap) start of 2018 before ICO bubble deflated. Hard fork required ≈51% mining capacity coordination among few large pools.

> **4.** DAO fork proved governance is human.
>
> **Q** What broke immutability?
>
> **A** 2016 DAO hack: $150M represented 14% of all ether. Community hard-forked to bail investors — ETH vs ETC split. ETC still trades ≈$4B cap. Cornell researchers blocked naive soft fork that would enable DDoS. Vitalik's human element vs Satoshi's untouched ≈$60B stash. Cornell researchers blocked naive soft fork that would enable DDoS — only hard fork or do nothing remained. Uniswap automated market maker replaced order-book trading on-chain.

> **5.** Congestion is bearish and bullish.
>
> **Q** Why hasn't Eth2 shipped?
>
> **A** Open-source foundation governance slow vs startup. Mid-2021 gas ≈$50–70 proves demand but pushes developers to Solana. Three mining pools ≈60% hash could hard-fork if aligned — incentive is ETH appreciation, not attack. Raspberry-Pi-level compute vs 100K TPS Eth2 target. EOS raised $4.2B ICO; Fred Wilson/Union Square frustration template for slow foundation governance. Acquired rated episode 3/5 — strong history, not definitive technical reference.
