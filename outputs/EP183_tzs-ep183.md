## Background

> In a 120-minute cross-show episode, Practical Investments co-founder Yongqing speaks with Wu Xiaobo on cycles. Four lenses: enterprise lifecycle (founder to bureaucracy), industry lifecycle (growth to consolidation), capital cycle (funding gluts and droughts), sentiment cycle (greed/fear overshoot). Four loss traps — buying high on sentiment, holding through fundamental decay, over-levering cyclicals, confusing price with value — recur across eras. Mutual funds historically peak-weight fashionable sectors at tops. Yongqing insists rational valuation must translate to expected return math; BRK.B embodies cycle patience, TSM flags global capital-cycle swings in fabs.

## Key Facts

> **F1** Four cycles: enterprise, industry, capital, sentiment — Yongqing and Wu map how all four stack; mistiming one cycle layer multiplies drawdowns. Enterprise lifecycle decay (bureaucracy) often coincides with industry maturity and sentiment peaks at year 10–15 of industry S-curves.

> **F2** Four loss traps: (1) sentiment-top buying, (2) holding through fundamental decay, (3) cyclical leverage, (4) price-value confusion. Mutual fund peak sector weights at tops are a measurable sentiment indicator — avoid mirroring crowd max allocation.

> **F3** Valuation discipline: intrinsic value is dynamic, not a static PE label — must connect to expected return under cycle-aware scenarios (12–15% hurdle common in Yongqing's framework). BRK.B Long as patient compounder; TSM Watch on semiconductor capex and capital-cycle turns.

## Key Insights

> **1.** Cycles stack; missing one layer is expensive.
>
> **Q** What is Yongqing's cycle framework?
>
> **A** Enterprise, industry, capital, and sentiment cycles interlock — buying at sentiment peak in a mature industry with tightening capital is the worst stack. Wu Xiaobo collaboration stresses teaching traps, not predicting dates. Map where you are on each cycle before sizing.

> **2.** Mutual fund peak weights are contrarian signals.
>
> **Q** How to use fund positioning data?
>
> **A** Historic pattern: funds max-weight hot sectors at tops — measurable sentiment extremity. Pair with four loss traps checklist before adding exposure. Expected return must improve as price falls, not just narratives flip bearish.

> **3.** Intrinsic value requires expected-return math.
>
> **Q** How differs from low-PE value labels?
>
> **A** Dynamic intrinsic value under cycle scenarios — cash flows, reinvestment, decay risk — must yield hurdle-beating expected return. Price cheap on PE can be value trap if enterprise lifecycle is decaying. BRK.B wins by surviving all four cycles with discipline.
